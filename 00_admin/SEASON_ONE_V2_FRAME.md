## The one choice v1 made (and v2 does not)

| v1 (retired) | v2 (active) |
|--------------|-------------|
| Season ends with Daniel/Bret **publishing** the public account | Season ends with the **hunt opening** — nothing published, nothing finished |
| Episode 8 = "The Inheritance" = archive complete | Episode 8 = **"The Count"** = they know there were **20**, the squad exists, **17 is a future tense** |
| Reads as series finale | Reads as **season finale / series pilot** |

**Series question after S1:** Not *what happened in the potato patch?* (we saw that).  
**It becomes:** *They got most of them. Who were the three that lived — and who protected them?*

|--------|-----|-------|
| **1** | Horner / Acord survivors | Dread → attack → cave → flight → **hunt ignites** |

## Season One v2 — eight episodes × five acts

Same count as v1. Different landing.

### Episode 1 — Mulberry Township
**Lane:** Dread before rupture.  
**Ends:** Chicken dinner table. Attack **not shown**.  
**Act 5 image:** Fire low. Children posted as lookouts. Tomorrow hanging over the house.

### Episode 2 — The Chicken Dinner
**Lane:** Supported / Tradition.  
**Covers:** Warning ignored. ~20 riders. Potato patch. William beaten. Homestead ransacked. Gold not found.  
**Ends:** Women moving William toward the ridge — not the house.

### Episode 3 — The Cave
**Lane:** Supported (tombstone + oral).  
**Covers:** Eight days in the overhang. Care under terror. "We already are."  
**Ends:** William still breathing. The county still silent.

### Episode 4 — The Refugee Train
**Lane:** Supported + oral.  
**Covers:** William dies Aug 10. Tombstone language forming in the family's mouth: *parties known*. Flight north. Road, ledger, child drawing in dirt.  
**Ends:** Family in motion. **No prosecution** mentioned only as absence — a blank where a warrant should be.

### Episode 5 — Company K
**Lane:** Proven (CMSR) + pattern map.  
**Covers:** Roster as **map of names** — who served where, who was home, who was AWOL in the August window. Audience starts to feel the **20** as a network, not a mob.  
**Ends:** Someone counts names on a muster sheet. The number **twenty** lands for the first time as a squad objective, not just a raid headcount.

### Episode 6 — The Silence
**Lane:** Inference + institutional weight.  
**Covers:** Horner POV on **John Fry Hill** — sheriff's office, Confederate command, same county. No indictment. Rumors. Amanda Hill as child in the background (not marriage story yet).  
**Ends:** A Horner reads the absence: *The law never came.* Sets up S2 without switching POV.

### Episode 7 — The Squad
**Lane:** Tradition (1942).  
**Covers:** Dr. John Turner Horner gathers men with terrain knowledge — **Wash Middleton, Fate Arnold, Jess Wilson**. Not revenge fantasy. **Accounting.**  
**Key dialogue spine:** "Twenty men in my father's field." / "How many names do we have?" / "Not enough." / "Then we learn the rest."  
**Ends:** Horses in the timber. Weapons checked. **First operation not yet shown.**

### Episode 8 — The Count
**Lane:** Tradition opens; primary record stays silent.  
**Covers:** **Cornfield ambush** (first operation only). Each man picks a target. Dr. Horner picks the **leader** — cannot shoot — throws his pistol. **Leader escapes.** At least one kill confirmed on-screen. Squad withdraws.  
**Does not cover:** Road killing. Dance house. Seventeen total. Marriage. Modern publish.

## Season One finale beat — Episode 8, Act 5

**Title card lane:** `FAMILY TRADITION — NOT PROVEN IN COURT RECORD`

### SCENE 1 — EXT. CORNFIELD — LATE DAY (1864 or early 1865)

Smoke low. Stubble. Men breathing hard between rows.

WASH MIDDLETON (30s, burly, quiet) reloads. FATE ARNOLD (small, iron) watches the tree line. JESS WILSON holds the horses — "wouldn't fight a chicken" energy, eyes sharp.

DR. JOHN TURNER HORNER (30s, physician, not a cinematic hero) stands over a body in the rows. Not triumph. **Arithmetic.**

```text
HORNER
That's one.

MIDDLETON
We had more names than rows.

HORNER
We had twenty riders in my father's field.
(beat)
How many is one.
```

He doesn't look at the body with satisfaction. He looks like a man starting a ledger.

### SCENE 2 — EXT. RIDGE ABOVE FIELD — SAME

A RIDER breaks from timber — the **LEADER** from Horner's line of sight. Fast. Knows the ground.

Horner raises a pistol. Hand shakes. Not cowardice — **the doctor's hand**.

He fires wide. Throws the pistol. The leader vanishes into the hill.

```text
ARNOLD
You let him go.

HORNER
I didn't let him go. I missed the world I was in.
```

**This man is Candidate #1 for the Three.** Audience may never learn if he's Stewart, the cornfield leader, or someone else.

### SCENE 3 — EXT. CORNFIELD EDGE — DUSK

Squad regroups. Blood on cuffs. No uniforms. No badges. No courthouse.

Middleton looks to Horner.

```text
MIDDLETON
How many left.

HORNER
(long beat)
Twenty in the field.
One in the rows.
(beat)
Nineteen.

ARNOLD
Then we're not done.

HORNER
No.
We're started.
```

### SCENE 4 — EXT. MOORE CEMETERY / HOMESTEAD RIDGE — NIGHT (optional intercut)

Permelia or Mary Ann at **William's fresh grave** or the homestead ruins. Wind. No music sting.

Tombstone carver's chisel sounds (off-screen). We do not hear the full inscription yet — only fragments:

```text
...Aug. 10, 1864...
...parties known...
```

### SCENE 5 — TAG (series frame — light touch)

**Choose one frame device; do not publish an archive.**

**Option A — 1942 porch (preferred):**  
Old PROF. JOHN TURNER HORNER tells a child: *"Dad had a squad… They killed seventeen out of the twenty bushwhackers."*  
Child: *"What happened to the other three?"*  
Old man: *"That's why the hills are still waiting."*  
**Smash to black.** No date stamp. No website. No QED.

**Option B — modern field:**  
Daniel/Bret at the potato-field GPS pin. Phone in pocket. **Does not open a publish screen.** Looks at the ridge.  
Voice (his, not narrating closure): *"Twenty names. Seventeen didn't get away in the telling. Three did."*  
**Smash to black.**

### FINAL LINE (either frame)

> **"The county never wrote it down. So somebody had to go back into the hills."**
