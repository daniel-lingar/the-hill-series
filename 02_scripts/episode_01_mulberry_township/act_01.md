# Episode 1 — Mulberry Township
## Act 1 — Cold Open + Act One

**Evidence lane:** Dread before rupture. Land, creek, ridge, homestead, neighbor knowledge, and the borrowed auger.

COLD OPEN

EXT. OZARK RIDGE — DAWN

Black.

Wind through leaves.

Water moving somewhere below.

Slowly, the image comes up.

A ridge in the Arkansas Ozarks.

Not grand.

Not postcard-beautiful.

Close. Steep. Heavy with timber.

Mist hangs low in the creek bottoms.

A narrow trace of road cuts through the trees, half-swallowed by brush.

Nothing moves.

Then —

A bird lifts from the trees.

The camera does not chase it.

It stays with the land.

The land is watching.

EXT. LITTLE MULBERRY CREEK — DAWN

Water slides over stone.

Cold.

Shallow in places.

Deep enough in others to slow a child down.

Two boys step carefully across.

JOHN SAMUEL ACORD, seven, carries a small bucket. He is too alert for his age.

CHRISTOPHER COLUMBUS ACORD, four, follows behind, trying to place his feet where John Samuel placed his.

Christopher slips.

John Samuel catches him by the sleeve.

No scolding.

Just a look.

Keep quiet.

Christopher nods.

They continue.

Above them, the ridge is dark.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — MORNING

A small rural homestead near the creek.

Rough timber.

Smoke from the chimney.

A fenced patch.

Tools leaned against the wall.

A field beyond.

The place is lived in, worked, vulnerable.

PERMELIA TURNER HORNER, early fifties, moves through the yard with the controlled economy of a woman who has no wasted motion left in her life.

MARY ANN BOEN HORNER, young, works near the doorway with food and cloth.

She looks up when the boys return.

John Samuel hands her the bucket.

Mary Ann notices the water sloshed out.

She almost says something.

Then sees Christopher’s face.

She doesn’t.

Inside the house, someone coughs.

A man’s voice.

SPENCER HORNER, older, steady, steps into view with a tool in his hand.

He looks toward the creek.

Then toward the ridge.

The look lasts a beat too long.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — MORNING

Dim interior.

A table.

A hearth.

A few plates.

A Bible or papers wrapped carefully and kept away from damp.

Nothing decorative unless it has survived use.

Mary Ann lays out food.

Permelia enters with the boys.

Christopher reaches for a piece of bread.

Permelia catches his wrist.

Not hard.

Just enough.

PERMELIA
Not yet.

Christopher withdraws.

John Samuel does not reach.

He has learned.

WILLIAM RILEY HORNER, thirty, enters from the back of the room. He moves like a man listening even when no one is speaking.

He looks at the boys.

WILLIAM
You see anybody?

John Samuel shakes his head.

Then, after a beat —

JOHN SAMUEL
Heard a horse.

Everyone stills.

MARY ANN
Where?

JOHN SAMUEL
Up past the bend.

William moves to the door.

Spencer watches him.

SPENCER
Road carries sound.

WILLIAM
So do ridges.

The line hangs.

Permelia looks at Spencer.

Spencer looks away first.

EXT. MODERN ARCHIVE TABLE / DANIEL’S WORKSPACE — DAY

A modern table.

Not slick.

Not cinematic.

Maps.

Photocopies.

A notebook.

A laptop pushed aside.

Old family names written in careful hand.

HORNER.

ACORD.

BOEN.

HILL.

A modern printed map shows Johnson County, Arkansas. Mulberry Township. Little Mulberry Creek.

DANIEL BRET LINGAR, modern day, stands over the table.

We do not make a meal of his face.

Hands first.

A finger traces the creek line.

Another finger marks a ridge.

A pencil circles a place.

DANIEL (V.O.)
This story does not start with a gunshot.

He studies the map.

DANIEL (V.O.)
It starts with land.

A pause.

DANIEL (V.O.)
And the first thing the land teaches you is that a map is not the same thing as knowing where you are.

Daniel looks from the modern map to an older land reference.

The neat lines do not explain the terrain.

Cut back.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — MORNING

Spencer sharpens or checks a hand tool.

The scrape of metal.

William stands nearby, watching the tree line.

SPENCER
You wear out your eyes staring at timber.

WILLIAM
Timber don’t bother me.

SPENCER
No?

WILLIAM
Men in it do.

Spencer stops sharpening.

Looks at him.

SPENCER
You hear something worth saying?

William does not answer right away.

WILLIAM
I hear enough not to like daylight.

A beat.

Spencer returns to the tool.

SPENCER
This is my house.

William looks toward the road.

WILLIAM
That may not matter anymore.

CUT TO BLACK.

TITLE CARD:

THE HILL

Episode One: Mulberry Township

ACT ONE

EXT. ROAD NEAR MILL / CREEK CROSSING — LATE MORNING

A rough local crossing.

Wagon ruts.

Mud.

Water.

A small mill structure or working place nearby, if production allows. If not, keep it to the crossing.

Spencer walks with a tool wrapped in cloth.

A man waits near a mule.

JESS WILSON, thirties or forties, ordinary enough to be more unsettling later.

Not a villain.

Not a monster.

A neighbor.

He has the face of someone who has worked outside his whole life and learned to keep thoughts behind his teeth.

JESS
Morning, Spencer.

SPENCER
Jess.

The men do not shake hands.

They know each other well enough not to need ceremony.

Jess nods toward the wrapped tool.

JESS
That the auger?

Spencer unwraps it.

An auger bit. Worn, cared for, useful.

SPENCER
It is.

Jess reaches for it.

Spencer does not immediately let go.

A small pause.

JESS
I’ll bring it back.

SPENCER
I know where you live.

Jess looks at him.

Then smiles faintly.

Not friendly.

Not unfriendly.

Something in between.

JESS
I know where you live too.

Spencer releases the tool.

The exchange is complete.

But something has entered the air.

Jess ties the tool onto his mule.

SPENCER
You heard anything from Clarksville?

JESS
Heard too much from everywhere.

SPENCER
That ain’t an answer.

JESS
Best kind lately.

Spencer studies him.

JESS (CONT'D)
Folks talking about who went north. Who signed. Who mustered. Who’s home when they ain’t supposed to be.

SPENCER
Folks talk.

JESS
That they do.

Jess tightens the strap.

A beat.

JESS (CONT'D)
Your boys still coming and going?

Spencer’s face changes slightly.

SPENCER
My family’s my business.

JESS
Used to be.

Jess clicks to the mule and starts away.

Spencer watches him go.

The tool disappears down the road with him.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — SAME DAY

John Samuel sits on a stump, scraping mud from his shoe with a stick.

Christopher plays near him, quiet.

Mary Ann shakes out cloth near the line.

Permelia works with cornmeal or vegetables.

Domestic labor.

No idle beauty.

William returns from the edge of the property.

Mary Ann sees him first.

MARY ANN
You went farther than you said.

WILLIAM
Not much.

MARY ANN
That ain’t an answer.

William almost smiles.

Doesn’t.

He looks toward the road.

WILLIAM
Acord place had smoke this morning.

Permelia hears the name.

Her hands slow.

MARY ANN
Whose?

WILLIAM
Couldn’t tell.

Permelia resumes work.

PERMELIA
Smoke means living.

WILLIAM
Sometimes.

The boys hear the adult silence after that.

John Samuel looks up.

Christopher keeps playing, but quieter.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — AFTERNOON

Food preparation.

Not a meal yet.

Work before a meal.

Mary Ann and Permelia inside.

The boys nearby.

William enters and takes a cup of water.

A beat.

MARY ANN
Francis sent word?

No one answers quickly.

That tells us enough.

PERMELIA
Not since last.

MARY ANN
Elizabeth would want to know.

Permelia stops.

Elizabeth’s name changes the room.

The boys feel it.

John Samuel looks at the floor.

Christopher looks at John Samuel.

PERMELIA
Elizabeth ain’t here to want.

Not cruel.

Just too true.

Mary Ann absorbs it.

MARY ANN
Those boys are.

Permelia looks toward John Samuel and Christopher.

Softens, but barely.

PERMELIA
I know where they are.

MARY ANN
Do we know where anybody is?

No answer.

William drinks.

The cup sounds loud when he sets it down.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — AFTERNOON

Spencer returns from the road.

William steps out to meet him.

WILLIAM
Who had it?

SPENCER
Jess.

William does not like that.

SPENCER (CONT'D)
Borrowed the auger.

WILLIAM
Why?

SPENCER
Likely to bore a hole.

William does not laugh.

WILLIAM
He ask after anybody?

Spencer looks at him.

SPENCER
Everybody asks after everybody now.

WILLIAM
That don’t answer me.

Spencer looks back toward the road.

SPENCER
He asked if my boys were still coming and going.

William’s jaw tightens.

WILLIAM
And?

SPENCER
I told him family was my business.

William turns away.

SPENCER (CONT'D)
Say what you mean.

WILLIAM
I mean he didn’t come for the auger.

Spencer does not answer.

Because somewhere inside him, he already knows.

EXT. MODERN WORKSPACE — DAY

Daniel sits with the map again.

Now another paper lies beside it.

A list of names.

Not a polished genealogy chart.

A working page.

Acord.

Horner.

Boen.

Company K.

Some names circled.

Some question marks.

Daniel writes:

NEIGHBOR KNOWLEDGE

Then below it:

INFORMATION MOVES BEFORE VIOLENCE

He stops.

Looks at the words.

DANIEL (V.O.)
The hard part is not proving that people died.

A pension reference.

A military record.

A tombstone note.

DANIEL (V.O.)
The hard part is proving how a county learns to know something and not say it out loud.

He turns a page.

DANIEL (V.O.)
In these families, information was never neutral. A name, a road, a return home, a man seen at the wrong hour — any of it could become dangerous.

Cut back.

EXT. SMALL WORK AREA / YARD — LATE AFTERNOON

William repairs something near the fence.

John Samuel watches.

Not helping.

Learning.

WILLIAM
You plan to stare that post straight?

John Samuel looks down.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
Hand me the wedge.

John Samuel does.

William works.

JOHN SAMUEL
Was my pa coming back?

William freezes for half a second.

Then keeps working.

WILLIAM
Your pa’s with his company.

JOHN SAMUEL
That ain’t what I asked.

William looks at him.

This boy is seven and already too old.

WILLIAM
No.

A painful honesty.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
It ain’t.

John Samuel nods, but the answer was not an answer.

William goes back to work.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
You hear a horse, you tell somebody.

JOHN SAMUEL
I did.

WILLIAM
You hear two, you tell faster.

John Samuel absorbs the instruction.

This is childhood now.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — DUSK

Mary Ann prepares supper.

Permelia works near the hearth.

Christopher has fallen asleep sitting up.

John Samuel sits awake.

William enters.

Spencer behind him.

The house holds them all, but barely.

MARY ANN
Lewis coming?

WILLIAM
Said he might.

SPENCER
Might means no.

PERMELIA
Might means might.

A beat.

Spencer sits.

Looks toward the door.

SPENCER
Tool should’ve been back by now.

No one answers.

That silence says everyone has been thinking the same thing.

EXT. ROAD ABOVE HOMESTEAD — DUSK

Jess Wilson moves along the ridge road, the borrowed tool tied to his mule.

He stops where the road gives him a view of the Horner place below.

Smoke rises from the chimney.

Children visible near the yard.

A man moving near the fence.

A woman at the doorway.

Jess watches.

Not long.

Long enough.

Then he continues down the road.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — NIGHT

Dim.

Food eaten.

Dishes not yet cleared.

Christopher asleep now.

John Samuel still awake.

Adults speak low.

PERMELIA
Mary Ann asked me today if they’d come here.

Spencer looks at her.

SPENCER
What’d you tell her?

PERMELIA
Nothing.

William looks at the door.

MARY ANN
You got an answer now?

No one answers.

Then Permelia, very quiet:

PERMELIA
They already know where here is.

The line lands.

No music.

Just the house.

Just the dark outside.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — NIGHT

The house lit from within.

The ridge black above it.

The road invisible now.

The borrowed tool has not returned.

A dog barks far away.

Then stops.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — NIGHT

John Samuel lies awake beside Christopher.

He hears the adults moving.

He hears the house breathe.

He hears something outside.

Maybe wind.

Maybe nothing.

His eyes stay open.

CUT TO BLACK.

END OF ACT ONE.
