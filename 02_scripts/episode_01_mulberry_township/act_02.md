ACT TWO

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — EARLY MORNING

Morning comes without peace.

The house is still there.

The ridge is still there.

The road is still unseen until someone steps onto it.

John Samuel is already awake.

He sits outside, knees pulled close, watching the tree line.

Christopher sleeps inside.

A rooster calls somewhere down the hollow.

The sound does not feel comforting.

It feels too loud.

Mary Ann steps out with a cup.

She sees John Samuel.

MARY ANN
You been out here long?

John Samuel shrugs.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
You hear something?

He shakes his head.

A beat.

JOHN SAMUEL
That’s why I’m listening.

Mary Ann does not know what to say to that.

She sits beside him.

Not close enough to make it soft.

Close enough so he is not alone.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — MORNING

Permelia works dough with hard, practiced hands.

Spencer sits at the table, repairing a strap.

William stands near the door.

Always near the door.

Mary Ann enters with the empty cup.

PERMELIA
He out there again?

MARY ANN
Watching.

No one asks who.

Everyone knows.

Spencer pulls the strap tight.

SPENCER
Boy needs chores.

WILLIAM
Boy has one.

Spencer looks up.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
You just don’t like it.

The room tightens.

Permelia keeps working.

PERMELIA
Eat first. Argue after.

No one laughs.

EXT. ROAD NEAR HORNER PLACE — LATER

A mule and small wagon come along the road.

A WOMAN in plain dress drives, face drawn.

Beside her, an older BOEN KINSMAN or NEIGHBOR sits with a long gun across his knees.

They stop short of the house.

They do not call out loudly.

That is the first warning.

William steps into the yard.

WILLIAM
Morning.

The kinsman nods.

BOEN KINSMAN
Morning.

Mary Ann comes to the doorway.

She knows them.

Her face changes.

MARY ANN
What happened?

The woman looks toward the children.

That answers before words do.

EXT. HORNER YARD — CONTINUOUS

Spencer and Permelia come out.

The visitors remain near the wagon.

No one wants to enter the yard with bad news.

BOEN KINSMAN
Word come up from the Acord side.

William stills.

Spencer looks at John Samuel, then back.

SPENCER
Francis?

BOEN KINSMAN
No.

The kinsman does not want to say the name.

He has to.

BOEN KINSMAN (CONT'D)
William Gordon.

Permelia closes her eyes.

Mary Ann puts a hand against the doorframe.

John Samuel watches every adult face.

SPENCER
When?

BOEN KINSMAN
March.

WILLIAM
March?

That is not just grief.

That is calculation.

The news has traveled late or been carried carefully.

BOEN KINSMAN
They say he come home to see after Martha and the children. Took corn to mill.

The woman’s voice enters, low.

WOMAN
She told one neighbor he was coming.

No one moves.

WOMAN (CONT'D)
One.

The word sits in the yard.

One.

The kinsman continues.

BOEN KINSMAN
Bushwhackers knew when he’d be back.

Christopher appears behind Mary Ann, rubbing sleep from his eyes.

Mary Ann quietly pushes him back inside.

WILLIAM
Who was the neighbor?

The woman does not answer.

The kinsman looks down.

BOEN KINSMAN
Ain’t named.

WILLIAM
Ain’t named, or ain’t spoken?

No answer.

That answer is enough.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — MOMENTS LATER

The wagon leaves.

Slow.

Quiet.

The house does not resume.

Spencer stands where he is.

William watches the road after the wagon disappears.

Permelia goes back inside.

Mary Ann follows.

John Samuel remains outside.

He has heard a new law:

One neighbor can be enough.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — DAY

Mary Ann kneels near Christopher, fixing his shirt.

Christopher squirms.

Mary Ann is too gentle with him.

Like she is trying to repair more than cloth.

At the table, Permelia cuts or folds something.

Sharp, efficient.

John Samuel sits in the corner.

He pretends not to listen.

Spencer enters.

SPENCER
Where’s William?

PERMELIA
Road.

SPENCER
Of course he is.

Permelia looks at him.

PERMELIA
You still think a road is just a road.

Spencer has no answer.

Mary Ann looks up.

MARY ANN
Martha told one neighbor.

No one responds.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
That’s all it took.

Permelia’s knife stops.

PERMELIA
Then mind what you tell.

Mary Ann looks toward John Samuel.

He heard.

Permelia knows he heard.

She does not soften the lesson.

EXT. TREE LINE ABOVE HORNER HOMESTEAD — DAY

William stands partly hidden, looking down the road.

He studies tracks.

Not like Fate Arnold will later.

Not as a scout.

As a man trying to measure danger without enough information.

Hoof marks.

Wagon ruts.

A boot print in soft mud.

He hears something behind him and turns fast.

It is LEWIS CASS HORNER, twenties, lean, alert, carrying himself like a man used to moving through risky country.

LEWIS
Easy.

William exhales.

WILLIAM
Don’t come up on a man quiet.

LEWIS
Then don’t stand where men come quiet.

A beat.

They understand each other.

EXT. TREE LINE — CONTINUOUS

Lewis looks down toward the house.

LEWIS
Who came?

WILLIAM
Boen kin. News on William Gordon.

Lewis absorbs it.

No visible shock.

Not because he does not care.

Because shock has become expensive.

LEWIS
How?

WILLIAM
Came home. Somebody told.

Lewis nods once.

LEWIS
That’s how they do it.

William looks at him.

WILLIAM
You know something?

LEWIS
I know too many folks know too much.

They look toward the house.

LEWIS (CONT'D)
Spencer still going in and out like land means law?

William says nothing.

LEWIS (CONT'D)
Tell him not to.

WILLIAM
You tell him.

LEWIS
He listens to me less.

William almost smiles.

It dies quickly.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — AFTERNOON

Lewis enters.

The children brighten slightly at seeing him, but not fully.

Nobody brightens fully anymore.

Permelia looks him over.

PERMELIA
You eat?

LEWIS
Not yet.

PERMELIA
Then sit.

He does.

Mary Ann puts food down.

Lewis does not start until Spencer enters and sits.

That old formality still exists.

Barely.

SPENCER
You look like a man bringing weather.

LEWIS
Weather’s already here.

No one eats for a beat.

LEWIS (CONT'D)
You don’t need to be up at the house in daylight.

Spencer looks at him.

SPENCER
This is the house.

LEWIS
I know what it is.

SPENCER
Then say what you mean.

LEWIS
I mean don’t make yourself easy to see.

Spencer sets his fork down.

SPENCER
I am not hiding from boys I’ve known since they had milk on their mouths.

Lewis leans forward.

Not angry.

Urgent.

LEWIS
Those boys are men now.

SPENCER
Men know better.

LEWIS
No. Men know where you live.

The table goes still.

That lands too close to Jess’s line, though Lewis does not know it.

William looks at Spencer.

Mary Ann looks at Permelia.

John Samuel looks at everybody.

SPENCER
I will not live like a thief in my own house.

Lewis holds his eyes.

LEWIS
That don’t matter no more.

Silence.

The line changes the room.

EXT. MODERN WORKSPACE — DAY

Daniel has a military service reference on the table.

Names.

Cards.

Dates.

A printed note: COMPANY K.

Another: BOEN — HIRAM.

Another: ACORD — WILLIAM G.

Daniel does not speak immediately.

He moves one paper beside another.

DANIEL (V.O.)
The record gives you categories.

A close view of words:

PRESENT.

ABSENT.

AWOL.

KILLED.

MUSTERED.

DANIEL (V.O.)
But the family lived inside what those categories could not say.

He writes:

KNOWN UNION FAMILIES

Then below it:

VISIBLE TO NEIGHBORS

DANIEL (V.O.)
A muster roll was not only army paperwork. In the wrong county, in the wrong season, it could become a list of who had chosen a side.

He pauses over Hiram Boen’s name.

Writes:

ABSENT — MAY 1864 TO JAN 1865

Then does not write anything else.

DANIEL (V.O.)
Some names answer questions. Some names become questions.

Cut back.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — LATE AFTERNOON

The meal is over.

No one has really eaten.

Lewis stands near the door.

Mary Ann clears plates.

Permelia scrapes leftovers into a small container.

Nothing wasted.

Spencer sits in hard silence.

William steps outside.

Lewis follows.

EXT. HORNER YARD — CONTINUOUS

William and Lewis stand just beyond the doorway.

WILLIAM
You hear on Hiram?

Lewis looks toward the house.

LEWIS
Boen?

William nods.

LEWIS
Not with his regiment, they say.

WILLIAM
Since when?

LEWIS
May. Maybe before.

WILLIAM
Where is he?

Lewis watches the ridge.

LEWIS
That’s the question.

William absorbs it.

WILLIAM
Mary Ann know?

LEWIS
Mary Ann knows enough already.

A beat.

From inside, Mary Ann’s voice calls softly to Christopher.

The domestic sound makes the men more afraid, not less.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — LATE AFTERNOON

John Samuel stands near the edge of the yard.

He sees Lewis and William speaking.

He sees them look toward the ridge.

Then he looks too.

The camera follows his gaze.

Nothing there.

Just trees.

But the trees now have meaning.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — EVENING

Mary Ann folds cloth.

Permelia checks the door latch.

Spencer notices.

SPENCER
Latch won’t stop a man with a gun.

PERMELIA
No.

She checks it anyway.

SPENCER watches her.

For the first time, we see his certainty weaken.

SPENCER
You think I’m wrong.

PERMELIA
I think you’re late.

That cuts deeper than anger.

Spencer looks toward the children.

SPENCER
They’re children.

PERMELIA
Not around here.

EXT. ROAD ABOVE HOMESTEAD — DUSK

Jess Wilson appears again at a distance.

No tool visible now.

He stands partly obscured by timber, watching the house.

A second RIDER joins him, face unseen.

They do not speak.

They look down toward the homestead.

Smoke.

Light.

Movement.

The house is a thing known.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — SAME

Lewis prepares to leave.

Permelia sees him gathering his things.

PERMELIA
Dark soon.

LEWIS
That’s why.

MARY ANN
Stay.

Lewis looks at her.

He wants to.

That is clear.

LEWIS
If I stay, I bring what’s following me.

No one argues.

William steps toward him.

WILLIAM
Where you headed?

LEWIS
Where I can hear before I’m heard.

He looks at Spencer.

LEWIS (CONT'D)
Stay off the open yard.

Spencer does not answer.

LEWIS (CONT'D)
I mean it.

Spencer rises.

SPENCER
I heard you.

LEWIS
No. You listened.

A beat.

LEWIS (CONT'D)
Ain’t the same.

Lewis exits.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — NIGHT

Lewis moves away from the house, taking the darker edge of the yard rather than the road.

John Samuel watches him go.

Lewis stops when he notices the boy.

LEWIS
You see something, you tell.

JOHN SAMUEL
I know.

LEWIS looks at him.

That answer hurts.

LEWIS
I wish you didn’t.

Lewis disappears into the dark.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — NIGHT

The family settles into uneasy night.

Christopher sleeps.

John Samuel does not.

Mary Ann sits beside William.

Spencer sits apart.

Permelia works even though there is no work left that needs doing.

The silence is full.

WILLIAM
Jess bring the auger back?

Spencer does not answer.

The answer is no.

Mary Ann closes her eyes.

Permelia keeps working.

EXT. ROAD ABOVE HOMESTEAD — NIGHT

The road is black.

The ridge is blacker.

Somewhere in the dark, a horse shifts weight.

Metal touches metal.

Quiet.

Then nothing.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — NIGHT

John Samuel’s eyes open.

He heard it.

He does not move yet.

He listens.

CUT TO BLACK.

END OF ACT TWO.
