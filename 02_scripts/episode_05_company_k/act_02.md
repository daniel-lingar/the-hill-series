# Episode 5 — Company K
## Act 2 — The Roster Is a Map

**Status:** GAP CLOSED — FIRST FULL SCRIPT PASS

**Evidence Lane:** Supported by Company K cluster research, including Horner/Acord connections and mixed record status. Dialogue and camp scenes dramatized.

---

### SCENE 1 — INT. COMMAND TENT — MORNING

Company K arrives as paper before it arrives as men.

Loose muster sheets. Casualty notes. Folded letters. Names copied from one hand to another until spelling becomes another battlefield.

The clerk lays them out on the command table.

The officer stands over them with the sergeant.

OFFICER
Start with every Johnson County name.

CLERK
That is not how company files are ordered.

OFFICER
Then disorder them.

The clerk does not enjoy the order, but he obeys.

The sergeant begins reading.

SERGEANT
Francis Marion Acord. Company K. Died of fever, Lewisburg, July twenty-eighth.

The name sits there.

SERGEANT (CONT'D)
William Riley Horner. Company K. Killed Johnson County, August tenth. Language varies.

OFFICER
Read the variations.

SERGEANT
Killed by rebel scout. Reported killed by guerrillas. Killed by parties known to his family.

The officer looks up.

OFFICER
That last one does not sound like army language.

CLERK
No, sir.

OFFICER
Good. Means it came from somewhere close.

The sergeant reads another.

SERGEANT
Pleasant Horner. Reported died August twelfth near Morrilton. Union service connection requires additional confirmation in full roll.

CLERK
So we mark uncertain?

OFFICER
We mark not settled.

SERGEANT
Lewis Cass Horner. Survived.

OFFICER
Put him in a separate column. Living witnesses matter.

The clerk draws columns.

DEAD.

SURVIVED.

UNCERTAIN.

KINSHIP.

LOCATION.

What looked like names begins to look like a shape.

---

### SCENE 2 — EXT. CAMP / WAGON SHADE — DAY

Mary Ann watches the tent from a distance.

John Samuel sits beside her with his scrap of paper: HOUSE, CAVE, ROAD, BELIEVE.

MARY ANN
Do not stare so hard at that tent you forget the sun.

JOHN SAMUEL
They are putting us on paper.

MARY ANN
They are putting pieces.

JOHN SAMUEL
Will pieces make it true?

Mary Ann thinks.

MARY ANN
Pieces can keep a lie from taking the whole field.

John Samuel looks at his paper.

JOHN SAMUEL
I wrote believe.

Mary Ann sees it.

MARY ANN
Believe who?

JOHN SAMUEL
Us.

Mary Ann cannot answer. She puts her hand on his shoulder.

---

### SCENE 3 — INT. COMMAND TENT — LATER

The officer has ordered a map pinned to a board.

Johnson County. Creeks. Roads. Rough lines. Home places marked from memory and report.

The clerk pins paper slips beside places.

HORNER HOME.

CAVE / OVERHANG — FAMILY ACCOUNT.

COMPANY K MEN.

ACORD CONNECTION.

The sergeant adds another note.

SERGEANT
Dr. John Turner Horner. Said to have organized Company K. Formal roll status unclear.

CLERK
If he organized but is not on roll, where does he go?

OFFICER
Where the paper fails.

The clerk looks at him.

OFFICER (CONT'D)
Make a column.

The clerk writes:

PAPER FAILURE / NEEDS CONFIRMATION.

SERGEANT
That column may get crowded.

OFFICER
Then at least it will be honest.

A private enters.

PRIVATE
Sir. There is talk around camp about names.

OFFICER
Talk is not a witness.

PRIVATE
No, sir. But it keeps repeating.

OFFICER
Which names?

PRIVATE
Middleton. Arnold. Wilson.

The officer looks at the map.

OFFICER
Do not pin those with the dead.

CLERK
Where then?

OFFICER
Heard.

The clerk writes another column:

HEARD / NOT PROVEN.

---

### SCENE 4 — EXT. CAMP EDGE — AFTERNOON

Permelia speaks with Sarah while mending cloth.

Sarah watches the command tent.

SARAH
Men like columns because columns do not cry.

PERMELIA
Columns keep fools from mixing every sorrow into one bucket.

SARAH
You trust them?

PERMELIA
No.

SARAH
Then why defend it?

PERMELIA
Because I have seen what happens when anger keeps records by itself.

Mary Ann approaches, hearing the last line.

MARY ANN
Anger remembers better than peace.

PERMELIA
Anger remembers loud. That is not the same as better.

Mary Ann sits.

MARY ANN
They are writing Middleton.

PERMELIA
They are writing heard.

MARY ANN
Is that enough?

PERMELIA
No.

MARY ANN
Then why does it feel like something?

PERMELIA
Because nothing is what they wanted.

---

### SCENE 5 — INT. COMMAND TENT — EVENING

The map is crowded now.

The officer, sergeant, and clerk stand back.

It is not a solution. It is a wound diagram.

OFFICER
Read it like you are explaining it to someone who does not want to believe you.

The sergeant takes a breath.

SERGEANT
In one kinship and neighborhood cluster tied to Johnson County Union service, we have multiple deaths or attacks within close time frame. Francis Marion Acord dies in service from fever. William Riley Horner is killed by irregulars or rebel scout after being found by family, according to family report. Spencer Horner killed at the homestead, date requires care. Pleasant Horner possibly dies in service or in connected movement, needs confirmation. Dr. John Turner Horner is named as organizer of Company K but the formal documentation is incomplete. Local names are repeated by oral report but not proven.

OFFICER
So what is it?

SERGEANT
A target pattern.

CLERK
Or a family unlucky enough to stand where everything broke.

The officer looks at the map.

OFFICER
Those may be the same thing.

---

### SCENE 6 — EXT. CAMP / NIGHT

John Samuel watches the lantern glow inside the command tent.

The camera moves inside the glow, to the map, to the columns.

PROVEN.

SUPPORTED.

HEARD.

QUESTION.

UNKNOWN.

The words are not spoken yet as doctrine, but their bones are there.

Mary Ann steps beside John Samuel.

JOHN SAMUEL
Will they go after them?

MARY ANN
I do not know.

JOHN SAMUEL
Do you want them to?

Mary Ann looks toward the dark beyond camp.

MARY ANN
I want William to stand up and tell me where his boots are.

John Samuel looks down.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
Since I cannot have that, I want the truth to stop being alone in our mouths.

Inside the tent, the officer takes one slip of paper and moves it into the center:

COMPANY K.

The roster is not background.

It is a map.

**END ACT TWO.**
