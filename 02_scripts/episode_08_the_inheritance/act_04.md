# Episode 8 — The Inheritance
## Act 4 — Built to Be Corrected

**Status:** GAP CLOSED — FIRST FULL SCRIPT PASS

**Evidence Lane:** Modern public-account scenes are dramatized from the project’s real method: source discipline, open correction, and refusal to turn descendants into defendants.

---

### SCENE 1 — INT. MODERN WORKSPACE — NIGHT

The screen glows in a dark room.

DANIEL / BRET sits in front of files, maps, cemetery photos, notes, and too many open tabs. He is not staged like a scholar in a clean library. He is a working man building a public account out of fragments after the rest of the day has already taken its share.

On the screen:

THE HILL — PUBLIC ACCOUNT

Sections appear one by one.

START HERE.

WHAT IS PROVEN.

WHAT IS SUPPORTED.

FAMILY TRADITION.

QUESTIONS.

UNKNOWN.

MAPS.

WHY IT MATTERS NOW.

He stops at FAMILY TRADITION.

His cursor hovers over the words:

WASH MIDDLETON AND FATE ARNOLD AVENGED 17 OF 20 BUSHWHACKERS.

He does not delete it.

He does not promote it.

He moves it into a marked box:

TRADITION — POWERFUL / UNCONFIRMED / NEEDS CASE-BY-CASE CORROBORATION.

DANIEL / BRET
(to himself)
Do not make hunger wear evidence clothes.

He types.

---

### SCENE 2 — ON SCREEN / PUBLIC NOTE DRAFT

Words form slowly.

DANIEL / BRET (V.O.)
This account is not built to punish descendants. Descendants are not defendants. This account is built to label the wound correctly so the living do not inherit silence and call it peace.

He stops.

Backspaces one phrase.

Types again.

DANIEL / BRET (V.O.)
No bloodline guilt. No bloodline purity. No bloodline exemption from truth.

He leans back.

That one stays.

---

### SCENE 3 — INT. 1870s HOUSE — NIGHT

John Samuel sits at a table with Amanda. The old paper is between them.

HOUSE.

CAVE.

ROAD.

BELIEVE.

A CHILD sits nearby, old enough to ask questions, young enough to ask them honestly.

CHILD
If grown men say a thing, does that make it true?

John Samuel looks at Amanda. She lets him answer.

JOHN SAMUEL
No.

CHILD
If you say it?

That hurts more.

JOHN SAMUEL
No.

The child studies him.

CHILD
But I’m supposed to listen to you.

JOHN SAMUEL
Listening is not the same as swallowing whole.

Amanda nods, quiet approval.

JOHN SAMUEL (CONT'D)
You do not believe a thing just because I need you to.

The child looks at the paper.

CHILD
Then why keep that?

John Samuel touches it with one finger.

JOHN SAMUEL
So I remember to tell it careful.

---

### SCENE 4 — INT. MODERN WORKSPACE — NIGHT

Daniel/Bret opens messages.

Some are supportive. Some are sharp.

MESSAGE 1: Why dig this up?

MESSAGE 2: You’re making one side look bad.

MESSAGE 3: My family name is in there. You better be careful.

He reads the last one twice.

DANIEL / BRET
Yes.

He types a reply, not sending yet.

DANIEL / BRET (V.O.)
Careful is not silence. Careful is not hiding the hard parts so nobody feels accused. Careful means I will not say proven when I mean heard. I will not say unknown when the record supports more. I will not make your ancestor carry what I cannot show. I will not clean mine either.

He pauses.

Adds:

DANIEL / BRET (V.O.)
The account is open for correction.

That line becomes a heading.

BUILT TO BE CORRECTED.

---

### SCENE 5 — EXT. MODERN JOHNSON COUNTY ROAD / LOCKED GATE — DAY

Daniel/Bret stands near a locked gate.

August in Arkansas. Heat shimmer. Cicadas loud enough to feel electrical. Green so thick it looks like the land is trying to hide every old thing under leaf and vine.

He holds a folder, but he does not cross the gate.

DANIEL / BRET (V.O.)
Family history does not cancel boundaries. A grave does not give me permission to trespass. A story does not make every locked gate mine to open.

He takes a photo from the road.

Marks in his notes:

ACCESS LIMITATION.

PRIVATE LAND.

NEEDS PERMISSION.

NOT VERIFIED ON FOOT.

He looks toward the hills.

DANIEL / BRET
As close as I can honestly stand.

The phrase is not poetic here. It is a rule.

---

### SCENE 6 — INT. COMMUNITY ROOM — EVENING

A small room. Folding tables. Local people. Some curious. Some defensive. Some related to names in the account. Some just there because history in a small county is never only history.

Daniel/Bret stands at the front with a screen behind him.

Not polished. Not pretending to be neutral in the lazy sense. Honest enough to show where his own blood enters the story.

On the screen:

PROVEN / SUPPORTED / TRADITION / QUESTION / UNKNOWN

DANIEL / BRET
I’m not here to give you a clean story. I do not have one.

The room listens.

DANIEL / BRET (CONT'D)
Some of the names in this story may be mine. Some may be yours. Some may be both before we are done. That does not make us enemies. It makes us responsible for how we handle what was left.

A MAN in the back raises his hand but does not wait to be called.

MAN
So are you saying our people were murderers?

The room tightens.

Daniel/Bret takes the hit without throwing one back.

DANIEL / BRET
I am saying the record shows Spencer Horner and William Riley Horner were killed in Johnson County in 1864, and family memory says the attackers were known. I am saying some names appear in tradition and suspicion. I am not saying every descendant of those names carries guilt. I am not saying tradition is proof. I am not erasing tradition either.

The man looks unsatisfied, but not dismissed.

DANIEL / BRET (CONT'D)
If I make my pain the final authority, I repeat the oldest mistake in the story.

That lands differently.

---

### SCENE 7 — INT. COMMUNITY ROOM — LATER

An older WOMAN comes to the table after the talk.

She carries a folder of her own.

OLDER WOMAN
My grandmother said a Middleton married into our line two generations later.

Daniel/Bret looks up.

DANIEL / BRET
Do you have documents?

OLDER WOMAN
Some. Not all.

DANIEL / BRET
Then we mark it that way.

She studies him.

OLDER WOMAN
You really don’t want to make it simple, do you?

DANIEL / BRET
I do. That’s why I don’t trust it.

She smiles despite herself and hands him a copy.

The table begins to change. Not into agreement. Into process.

People bring names, dates, corrections, objections, stories they were told in kitchens and at graves.

Daniel/Bret writes categories on sticky notes:

DOCUMENTED.

FAMILY STORY.

CHECK LAND RECORD.

VERIFY CEMETERY.

DO NOT CLAIM YET.

The public account is not a monument. It is a table with room for correction.

---

### SCENE 8 — EXT. CEMETERY — SUNSET

Daniel/Bret stands among stones.

The community room is behind him now. The files are heavier. Not because they answered everything, but because other people have started touching the weight.

DANIEL / BRET (V.O.)
The goal is not to punish the dead. The goal is to stop making the living inherit an unlabeled wound.

He looks at one stone, then another.

DANIEL / BRET
I told them it was mine.

A beat.

DANIEL / BRET (CONT'D)
And not only mine.

The sun drops low through trees. August light thick and gold.

He closes the folder.

DANIEL / BRET (V.O.)
That may be the hardest part of inheritance — holding it without owning it wrong.

The camera holds on the folder title:

THE HILL — PUBLIC ACCOUNT — OPEN DRAFT.

Blackout.

**END ACT FOUR.**
