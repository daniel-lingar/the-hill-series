ACT THREE

INT. HORNER HOUSE — NIGHT

Black inside the room.

Not full dark.

The fire gives just enough shape to make fear visible.

William stands by the door.

Spencer sits at the table with the rifle across his lap.

Permelia is awake in the chair.

Mary Ann lies beside Christopher but does not sleep.

John Samuel sits low beneath the window, knees pulled in, listening.

The house is quiet in the way a held breath is quiet.

Outside, the ridge is silent.

Too silent.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — NIGHT

The riders are gathered farther back in the trees.

No fire.

No speaking above a murmur.

Horses shift.

Leather creaks.

Jess Wilson stands apart from the others, the borrowed auger in his hand now.

He looks at it.

The young rider watches him.

YOUNG RIDER
Why keep that thing?

Jess looks over.

JESS
Because he’ll know it.

YOUNG RIDER
Who?

Jess looks toward the hidden house below.

JESS
Spencer.

The young rider understands less than he wants to.

YOUNG RIDER
You want him to know?

Jess ties the auger back to his saddle.

JESS
I want him to step out.

The second rider hears that and smiles.

Jess does not.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — NIGHT

John Samuel hears something.

Not a horse.

Metal.

Small.

Far.

He looks toward William.

JOHN SAMUEL
The tool.

William turns.

WILLIAM
What?

JOHN SAMUEL
I heard the tool.

Spencer looks up.

The words hit him harder than “horse” would have.

SPENCER
You don’t know that.

John Samuel looks at him.

JOHN SAMUEL
I know what missing sounds like.

No one speaks.

That line is too much for a child.

And true.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — LATE NIGHT

The house sits alone.

The field opens beyond it.

The wooded hillside beyond that.

The cave country farther still, unseen.

The camera holds long enough for the geography to enter the body.

House.

Field.

Woods.

Distance.

No one says it.

But this is the route that will matter.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — LATE NIGHT

Mary Ann shifts, trying to keep Christopher asleep.

He murmurs.

CHRISTOPHER
Creek.

MARY ANN
Sleep.

CHRISTOPHER
I want water.

Mary Ann looks toward the water bucket.

Almost empty.

She looks at Permelia.

Permelia sees.

Neither says anything.

Water will be needed by morning.

That fact becomes another threat.

William notices the bucket.

WILLIAM
I’ll go before first light.

MARY ANN
No.

WILLIAM
We need water.

MARY ANN
Then we need it after.

WILLIAM
After what?

Mary Ann cannot answer.

Because the answer is:

after whatever is coming.

Spencer stands.

SPENCER
I’ll go.

William turns on him.

WILLIAM
You won’t.

SPENCER
I am not asking permission in my own house.

PERMELIA
Then hear command.

Spencer stops.

Permelia has not raised her voice.

She does not need to.

PERMELIA (CONT'D)
No one goes out till light shows the whole yard.

Spencer holds her eyes.

Then sits.

Not beaten.

Contained.

EXT. MODERN WORKSPACE — NIGHT

Daniel’s table under lamplight.

He studies a rough sketch of the site:

HOUSE

FIELD

WOODED HILLSIDE

CAVE AREA

CREEK

RIDGE ROAD

His pencil moves between them.

DANIEL (V.O.)
When you read the account later, the places sound separate.

He marks each point.

DANIEL (V.O.)
The house. The field. The hillside. The cave.

A pause.

DANIEL (V.O.)
But on the land, they are not separate. They are decisions a person has to make while terrified.

He draws no clean arrow.

Only a broken line.

DANIEL (V.O.)
A route is not a route until someone has to run it.

Cut back.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — PRE-DAWN

The night thins.

Not morning yet.

A gray line at the window.

John Samuel’s head has dipped.

He jerks awake before he fully sleeps.

William sees it.

WILLIAM
Come here.

John Samuel crawls closer.

William takes off his coat and puts it around him.

JOHN SAMUEL
I ain’t cold.

WILLIAM
Didn’t ask.

John Samuel allows it.

A small kindness.

Almost fatherly.

Spencer watches.

Something passes across his face — grief for what the boy has lost, and for what he is still losing.

SPENCER
Your pa was stubborn too.

John Samuel looks at him.

SPENCER (CONT'D)
Francis.

The name opens the room.

Mary Ann turns slightly.

Permelia closes her eyes.

JOHN SAMUEL
Was?

Spencer realizes the word before anyone else does.

Was.

Not is.

No one rescues him.

Spencer swallows.

SPENCER
Is.

A lie.

A mercy.

A failure.

John Samuel does not believe it.

But he lets Spencer keep it.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — PRE-DAWN

Jess and the riders mount.

No haste.

They are not charging.

They are arranging.

One rider moves down a side route.

Another stays high.

The young rider hesitates.

SECOND RIDER
Move.

He moves.

Jess remains still, watching the first light open the yard below.

The auger hangs at his saddle.

Visible.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — DAWN

Permelia stands.

The room follows her movement because she is morning now.

PERMELIA
Water.

No one likes the word.

The bucket is nearly empty.

William rises.

Spencer rises too.

PERMELIA (CONT'D)
William.

Spencer looks at her.

SPENCER
You heard yourself.

PERMELIA
I heard.

SPENCER
You choose him?

Permelia turns to him.

PERMELIA
I choose the one who knows to come back.

That cuts.

Spencer takes it.

William takes the bucket.

Mary Ann steps toward him.

MARY ANN
Don’t go alone.

William looks at John Samuel.

Then thinks better of it.

WILLIAM
No.

John Samuel hears the denied assignment.

It hurts and relieves him at once.

EXT. HORNER YARD — DAWN

William steps out with the bucket.

Slow.

No sudden movement.

He scans:

ridge

field

shed

road

fence

tree line

Nothing.

The yard is cold.

The chicken bones from yesterday’s meal sit in a scrap pail near the door.

A fly already there.

William walks toward the creek.

From the ridge, unseen eyes follow him.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

Mary Ann watches through the side of the window, careful not to show herself.

Permelia watches Mary Ann.

Spencer stands at the door but does not open it.

John Samuel sits up straight.

Christopher wakes.

CHRISTOPHER
Where’s William?

MARY ANN
Water.

CHRISTOPHER
I wanted to go.

No one answers.

Christopher begins to understand that wanting has become useless.

EXT. CREEK — DAWN

William fills the bucket.

Fast enough to show fear.

Slow enough not to look panicked.

He listens.

Water.

Birds.

A horse somewhere.

He stops.

Looks up.

Nothing.

Then, across the water, a shape moves behind timber.

William holds still.

The shape is gone.

He lifts the bucket and starts back.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — DAWN

Jess sees William returning.

SECOND RIDER
Take him now.

Jess watches William’s path.

JESS
Not there.

SECOND RIDER
Why?

Jess points with his chin.

The yard.

The house.

The old man inside.

JESS
He gets back, they all know. He don’t, they scatter.

SECOND RIDER
So?

JESS
We need them looking one way.

The second rider studies the house.

Then smiles.

SECOND RIDER
Old man.

Jess does not smile.

EXT. HORNER YARD — DAWN

William returns with water.

Mary Ann opens the door just enough.

He steps inside.

She shuts it behind him.

The family exhales as one body.

Spencer sees it.

SPENCER
You all act like he crossed a battlefield.

William sets down the bucket.

WILLIAM
I did.

Spencer has no answer.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — MORNING

The water is poured.

Small cups.

Careful portions.

Christopher drinks too fast.

Mary Ann lowers the cup.

MARY ANN
Slow.

The same word from the dinner.

Slow.

Everything now means something else.

John Samuel watches William’s boots.

Mud.

A mark.

A broken twig caught in the cuff.

JOHN SAMUEL
You went through brush.

WILLIAM
A little.

JOHN SAMUEL
Why?

William looks at him.

He could lie.

He does not.

WILLIAM
Somebody was near the creek.

The room changes.

Spencer stands.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
Sit down.

Spencer does not.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
Sit down.

This time it is not son to father.

It is one endangered man to another.

Spencer sits.

EXT. MODERN WORKSPACE — MORNING

Daniel looks at the phrase:

KNOWN TO HIS FAMILY

Then at:

THEY KNEW THE HOUSE

He writes:

THEY ALSO KNEW THE ROUTINES

DANIEL (V.O.)
A raid does not begin when men ride into a yard.

A beat.

DANIEL (V.O.)
It begins when they learn when you fetch water. When you eat. Who stands at the door. Which child watches. Which man will step outside first.

He stops writing.

DANIEL (V.O.)
By the time violence arrives, it has already studied the house.

Cut back.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — LATER MORNING

The sun is higher.

Not warm.

Spencer steps outside before anyone stops him.

Just one step.

Then another.

William appears in the doorway.

WILLIAM
Pa.

Spencer does not turn.

SPENCER
I know.

WILLIAM
Then come back in.

Spencer looks across the yard.

The ridge.

The road.

The field.

His house.

His land.

SPENCER
If a man can’t stand in his own yard, what’s left?

William steps outside too.

WILLIAM
Us.

Spencer turns.

The word lands.

Us.

Not land.

Not pride.

People.

Mary Ann stands behind William.

Permelia behind her.

The boys behind them.

Spencer sees the line of the living.

For one breath, he might choose them.

Then a sound comes from the shed.

A scrape.

All heads turn.

EXT. SHED — CONTINUOUS

The shed door moves slightly in the wind.

Or not wind.

A shadow passes behind it.

William raises the rifle.

Spencer moves before him.

WILLIAM
Pa.

Spencer crosses the yard.

Not far.

Too far.

William follows.

Mary Ann pulls the children back.

Permelia steps into the doorway.

The shed door creaks.

Spencer reaches it.

Throws it open.

Nothing.

Only tools.

Hanging rope.

A broken handle.

An empty space where an auger might have been.

Spencer sees the empty peg.

The missing tool again.

Then from above:

A horse snorts.

Spencer turns toward the ridge.

For one second he sees movement.

A man in the timber.

Maybe Jess.

Maybe not.

Then gone.

William reaches him.

WILLIAM
Back.

Spencer does not resist this time.

They move toward the house.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — SAME

Jess watches.

The second rider mutters.

SECOND RIDER
You had him.

Jess looks at Spencer moving back to the house.

JESS
No.

SECOND RIDER
Looked like him to me.

JESS
Looked like a warning.

Jess turns.

JESS (CONT'D)
Next time he comes farther.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — LATE MORNING

Spencer and William enter.

Mary Ann shuts the door.

Permelia does not ask what they saw.

She saw enough on their faces.

John Samuel looks at Spencer.

JOHN SAMUEL
Was it him?

Spencer looks at the boy.

He knows who “him” means.

Jess.

Neighbor.

Borrower.

Known man.

Spencer cannot say yes.

Cannot say no.

SPENCER
It was somebody.

John Samuel nods.

That is almost worse.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — NOON

The family sits in the heat of the room.

The door closed.

The window cracked only slightly.

Air does not move.

Christopher sweats.

Mary Ann wipes his face.

Permelia looks at the food left from the previous meal.

No one wants to eat.

Bodies still need food.

She begins preparing plates.

Spencer watches her.

SPENCER
You making dinner?

PERMELIA
I’m feeding people.

SPENCER
Now?

PERMELIA
If I knew when death was coming, I’d schedule around it.

No one says anything.

She sets plates down.

The family gathers because there is nothing else to do.

This is not normal.

This is defiance without a speech.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — NOON

The riders wait.

The house is closed now.

Harder to read.

Jess watches smoke from the chimney.

The young rider looks pale.

YOUNG RIDER
They’re eating again?

SECOND RIDER
People do that before they die.

The young rider looks at him, disturbed.

Jess hears.

JESS
Shut up.

The second rider grins.

SECOND RIDER
Soft after all.

Jess turns on him.

JESS
No.

A beat.

JESS (CONT'D)
Careless talk gets remembered.

The second rider stops smiling.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

The family eats again, but this is not the chicken dinner as title-image.

This is survival food.

Small portions.

Sweat.

Fear.

A house turned inward.

John Samuel chews slowly.

Christopher watches him and copies.

Spencer looks toward the door.

William looks toward the window.

Permelia looks at everybody.

Mary Ann touches the table with one hand, as if steadying the whole house.

Then —

A distant call.

Not words.

A bird maybe.

A man maybe.

Everyone stops.

William stands.

The call does not repeat.

No one resumes eating.

The food sits between them.

Alive people around a table, waiting to learn whether they are still alive.

CUT TO BLACK.

END OF ACT THREE.
