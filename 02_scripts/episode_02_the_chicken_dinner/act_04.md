ACT FOUR

INT. HORNER HOUSE — AFTERNOON

Black.

Then the room again.

Same table.

Same food.

Same people.

But the air has changed.

No one pretends anymore.

The family is not waiting for something to happen.

They are waiting for the thing that has already begun to finish arriving.

William stands near the door.

Spencer sits at the table, rifle across his knees.

Permelia wipes Christopher’s face with a damp cloth.

Mary Ann watches the window without looking directly at it.

John Samuel sits low beneath the sill.

He listens like listening is work.

Outside, the distant call does not repeat.

That makes it worse.

EXT. HORNER YARD — AFTERNOON

The yard is still.

A scrap of cloth moves on the line.

The shed door hangs slightly open from earlier.

The field beyond the house is wide and pale.

The potato patch waits in the middle distance.

The wooded hillside stands farther back.

Close enough to promise safety.

Far enough to kill a man before he reaches it.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

Christopher whispers.

CHRISTOPHER
Can we talk now?

Mary Ann almost breaks.

She smooths his hair.

MARY ANN
Soft.

CHRISTOPHER
Why?

Mary Ann looks to Permelia.

Permelia answers without turning.

PERMELIA
Because sound travels when people are listening for it.

Christopher takes that in.

A child learning physics through terror.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — SAME

Jess Wilson crouches beside a tree.

The second rider and the young rider stand behind him.

Two more riders are farther back, mostly hidden.

Jess watches the house.

SECOND RIDER
They ain’t coming out.

JESS
They will.

SECOND RIDER
You been saying that.

Jess looks toward the field.

JESS
A house gets smaller the longer fear sits in it.

The second rider does not understand.

Jess does.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — AFTERNOON

Spencer stands.

William’s head turns.

WILLIAM
Don’t.

SPENCER
I ain’t stepped.

WILLIAM
You’re thinking it.

SPENCER
Thinking still allowed?

WILLIAM
Not if it gets you killed.

Spencer takes that.

He looks toward the table.

The chicken bones.

The bread.

The water.

The children.

The women.

SPENCER
This is no way to live.

PERMELIA
We ain’t living. We’re getting through.

Spencer looks at her.

The words land.

Getting through.

Not living.

EXT. MODERN WORKSPACE — DAY

Daniel’s table.

The map from the prior acts.

The phrase:

A HOUSE UNDER THREAT CHANGES BEFORE ANYONE TOUCHES IT.

Daniel adds:

GETTING THROUGH IS NOT LIVING

He sits with that for a moment.

DANIEL (V.O.)
The record writes death like a point on a line.

A military card.

A tombstone reference.

A date.

DANIEL (V.O.)
But before the date, there is time.

He marks the homestead.

DANIEL (V.O.)
Hours where a family has not died yet, but has already lost the world that made ordinary life possible.

Cut back.

EXT. CREEK ROAD — AFTERNOON

The local man from earlier has stopped farther down the road.

He stands with his mule.

He can no longer see the Horner house clearly.

He can still see smoke.

He looks back.

His mouth tightens.

He reaches into his coat and pulls out something small — maybe a scrap of cloth, maybe nothing useful at all.

A signal?

A warning?

He cannot decide.

He hears a horse behind him.

He hides the object.

Keeps moving.

Fear wins again.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — AFTERNOON

Mary Ann stands.

WILLIAM
Where you going?

MARY ANN
Nowhere. I’m standing.

She is.

But standing feels like movement now.

She looks at the water bucket.

Half full.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
We don’t have enough.

WILLIAM
We have enough.

MARY ANN
For what?

No one answers.

Enough for supper?

Enough for night?

Enough for hiding?

Enough for wounds?

Enough for dying?

The bucket sits between them like a question.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — SAME

The young rider watches through the trees.

YOUNG RIDER
There’s women in there.

SECOND RIDER
You counted that already.

YOUNG RIDER
I know.

SECOND RIDER
Then stop saying it.

The young rider looks sick.

Jess hears but keeps his eyes on the house.

JESS
You want to leave?

The young rider looks at him.

No answer.

JESS (CONT'D)
Then don’t waste breath telling yourself you’re better than the thing you’re still here for.

The young rider looks away.

That lands harder than a threat.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — LATE AFTERNOON

The room grows hotter.

No one wants to open the door.

No one wants the door closed.

Christopher has fallen asleep against Mary Ann.

John Samuel is awake enough for both of them.

Spencer looks at him.

SPENCER
Come here.

John Samuel hesitates.

Then comes.

Spencer reaches into his pocket.

Pulls out a small object.

Maybe a coin.

Maybe a carved bit of wood.

Maybe nothing valuable.

He presses it into John Samuel’s hand.

JOHN SAMUEL
What’s this?

SPENCER
Something to keep.

JOHN SAMUEL
Why?

Spencer almost says something too sentimental.

He stops himself.

SPENCER
Because I said.

John Samuel looks at the object.

Then at Spencer.

He knows adults give things when they are afraid of leaving.

He closes his fist around it.

EXT. FIELD EDGE — SAME

A rider moves low along the far edge of the field.

Not close.

Testing sightlines.

From the house, he might be seen.

From the ridge, he is covered.

He pauses near the potato patch.

Looks toward the wooded hillside.

Measures the distance with his eyes.

Then backs away.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

John Samuel’s head turns.

He saw movement.

Not enough.

Maybe enough.

JOHN SAMUEL
Field.

William moves to him.

WILLIAM
Where?

John Samuel points low.

Not out the window.

Toward a line only he has been watching.

JOHN SAMUEL
There.

William looks.

Nothing.

Spencer rises.

WILLIAM
Stay.

Spencer ignores him and comes to the window.

Mary Ann pulls Christopher closer.

Permelia steps to the side of the room where she can see the door and both boys.

The whole house becomes a mechanism of fear.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
I don’t see it.

JOHN SAMUEL
It moved.

WILLIAM
Man?

John Samuel’s eyes stay on the field.

JOHN SAMUEL
Maybe.

SPENCER
Maybe ain’t enough to start shooting shadows.

William looks at him.

WILLIAM
It’s enough to stop standing in windows.

He pulls Spencer back.

Spencer lets him.

Another old rule dying.

EXT. MODERN WORKSPACE — AFTERNOON

Daniel marks:

CHILD WITNESS

Then:

SAW BEFORE OTHERS BELIEVED

He stops.

DANIEL (V.O.)
The children were not passive witnesses.

A beat.

DANIEL (V.O.)
They were part of the warning system because the adults had run out of safe ways to know what was coming.

He underlines:

HERE THEY COME GRANDPAP

But does not speak it yet.

The line is waiting.

Cut back.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — EVENING

The light begins to fall.

Again.

This is the second evening of dread.

The family has made it through another day.

No one feels saved.

Permelia starts gathering scraps.

Mary Ann takes the plates without being asked.

The table empties slowly.

Spencer watches the doorway.

William watches Spencer.

John Samuel watches the window.

Three generations watching different dangers.

Christopher wakes.

CHRISTOPHER
Is it morning?

No one speaks.

Mary Ann kisses his hair.

MARY ANN
Not yet.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — EVENING

Jess and the riders reposition again.

They move with more purpose now.

Not toward the house.

Around it.

One rider takes the upper road.

One slips toward the lower timber.

The young rider follows Jess.

YOUNG RIDER
I thought you said morning.

JESS
Morning starts before light.

The young rider looks toward the house.

YOUNG RIDER
They know.

Jess nods.

JESS
Knowing wears a man down too.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — EVENING

William hears distant movement.

Not clear.

Enough.

WILLIAM
They’re shifting.

Spencer stands.

SPENCER
Where?

William listens.

WILLIAM
Around.

That word lands.

Around.

Not above.

Not away.

Around.

Permelia moves to Mary Ann.

PERMELIA
Get what you need close.

MARY ANN
For what?

Permelia looks at her.

No answer.

Mary Ann understands.

She quietly gathers:

cloth

water

a small knife

blanket

bread

Not packing.

Preparing.

That distinction hurts.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — LATER

A tiny domestic sequence under terror.

Mary Ann folds cloth strips and tucks them near the hearth.

Permelia fills a small cup and sets it where it can be grabbed.

William checks the rifle again.

Spencer checks his own.

John Samuel moves Christopher’s sleeping place away from the window.

Christopher complains half asleep.

CHRISTOPHER
Stop.

JOHN SAMUEL
You’re too close.

CHRISTOPHER
To what?

John Samuel does not answer.

He keeps pulling the blanket.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — NIGHTFALL

The house from outside.

Small.

Lit.

Surrounded by land.

The ridge above.

The field beside.

The creek below.

The road hidden.

The riders unseen now.

But the audience knows they are there.

The land has become a closed hand.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — NIGHTFALL

Spencer sits.

Then stands.

Then sits again.

He cannot bear the waiting.

Permelia sees the motion.

PERMELIA
You’ll wear a hole in the floor.

SPENCER
Better than wearing one in my mind.

PERMELIA
Too late for that.

The line is harsh.

True.

They look at each other.

Long marriage in one look.

Fear has made them crueler and closer at the same time.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — NIGHT

Jess ties the auger to a low branch near the road where it can be seen if someone comes up.

Not returned.

Displayed.

A lure.

A message.

A borrowed object turned into bait.

The second rider watches.

SECOND RIDER
You think he’ll come for it?

Jess looks toward the house.

JESS
No.

A beat.

JESS (CONT'D)
I think it’ll make him want to.

That is enough.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — NIGHT

A sound outside.

Metal.

Small.

John Samuel’s eyes snap open.

He looks at Spencer.

Spencer heard it.

William heard it.

No one moves.

Another sound.

The auger, faintly, in the wind.

A borrowed tool calling from the dark.

Spencer stands.

PERMELIA
No.

Spencer does not move toward the door.

But every part of him wants to.

WILLIAM
Pa.

Spencer’s hand curls.

The humiliation is unbearable.

A man’s tool.

A neighbor’s insult.

A trap.

He knows all three.

Knowing does not make it easier.

SPENCER
I hear it.

WILLIAM
Then hear what it is.

A beat.

Spencer sits back down.

Slowly.

The family survives that minute.

Only that minute.

EXT. MODERN WORKSPACE — NIGHT

Daniel writes:

THE TOOL RETURNS AS BAIT

Then stops.

Crosses out RETURNS.

Writes:

THE TOOL DOES NOT RETURN.

IT CALLS.

DANIEL (V.O.)
Objects remember differently than people.

A beat.

DANIEL (V.O.)
A borrowed tool can become proof of trust.

Or proof that trust has been turned against you.

He looks at the map.

DANIEL (V.O.)
That is the horror of known men.

Cut back.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — NIGHT

The house settles into another kind of silence.

Not relief.

Not safety.

A silence after resisting bait.

Mary Ann looks at Spencer.

She sees what it cost him not to move.

For the first time, she feels pity for him.

Not because he is weak.

Because he is trapped between the world he believed in and the world that is outside his door.

Christopher sleeps.

John Samuel does not.

William stands.

Permelia watches the fire burn low.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — DEEP NIGHT

The auger hangs from the branch.

Moving slightly.

A tiny creak.

Below, the house remains lit.

Above, riders wait.

The attack still has not come.

That is the violence of the act.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — DEEP NIGHT

John Samuel whispers to himself, almost without sound:

JOHN SAMUEL
Morning starts before light.

He heard it from somewhere?

Or understood it on his own?

Either way, it is now inside him.

William looks over.

WILLIAM
What?

John Samuel shakes his head.

JOHN SAMUEL
Nothing.

William does not believe him.

No one believes “nothing” anymore.

CUT TO BLACK.

END OF ACT FOUR.
