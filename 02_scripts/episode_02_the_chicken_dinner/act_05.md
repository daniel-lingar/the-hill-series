ACT FIVE

INT. HORNER HOUSE — PRE-DAWN

Dark.

The fire is almost gone.

The room is crowded with breath.

No one is truly asleep.

William stands at the door, rifle in hand.

Spencer sits at the table, head lowered, eyes open.

Permelia sits near the hearth.

Mary Ann lies beside Christopher, one arm around him.

John Samuel sits low beneath the window.

Listening.

The faint metal sound from the auger has stopped.

That does not feel like mercy.

It feels like the world holding still.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — PRE-DAWN

The auger hangs from the branch.

Still now.

Jess Wilson cuts it down.

No words.

He ties it back to his saddle.

The second rider checks his pistol.

The young rider watches his own hands.

They shake once.

He hides them.

More riders move in the trees.

Not many.

Enough.

Jess looks down toward the Horner house.

The faintest gray edge appears behind the ridge.

Morning starts before light.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — PRE-DAWN

John Samuel’s eyes shift.

He hears something change outside.

Not louder.

Closer.

He turns toward William.

JOHN SAMUEL
They stopped waiting.

William looks at him.

WILLIAM
How do you know?

John Samuel does not answer.

He does not know how to explain the sound of a thing deciding.

Spencer slowly lifts his head.

Permelia stands.

Mary Ann wakes fully.

Christopher stirs.

CHRISTOPHER
Morning?

No one answers.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — DAWN

The yard in early gray.

The field pale.

The potato patch dark with dew.

The wooded hillside beyond.

A route visible now:

house to yard

yard to field

field to potatoes

potatoes to timber

Almost safety.

Almost.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — DAWN

Permelia moves first.

She adds wood to the fire because bodies still need heat.

Mary Ann starts to rise.

PERMELIA
Stay with him.

Mary Ann settles back beside Christopher.

William steps toward the window.

Spencer stands.

SPENCER
I’m going out.

WILLIAM
No.

SPENCER
I am.

WILLIAM
They want that.

SPENCER
Maybe.

WILLIAM
Pa.

Spencer turns.

There is no anger left in him now.

Only a man tired of being handled by fear.

SPENCER
A man cannot wait forever inside his own walls.

William looks at him.

WILLIAM
He can if waiting keeps him alive.

Spencer’s face changes.

A bitter tenderness.

SPENCER
That living to you?

William has no answer.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — DAWN

The riders begin moving.

Not charging.

Descending.

Slow through the trees.

Hooves in leaf litter.

Leather creak.

Breath from horses.

Jess leads, but not proudly.

The second rider follows.

The young rider behind them.

The house below is visible through gaps.

Smoke starting.

A family inside.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

John Samuel hears them.

This time there is no maybe.

His body goes still.

William sees.

WILLIAM
Where?

John Samuel points toward the ridge.

JOHN SAMUEL
Above the road.

William moves to the door.

Mary Ann pulls Christopher tighter.

Christopher wakes confused.

CHRISTOPHER
What?

Mary Ann covers his mouth gently.

Not hard.

Enough.

Permelia steps toward the window but stays out of sight.

Spencer picks up his rifle.

William grabs his arm.

WILLIAM
Do not step out.

Spencer looks at William’s hand on him.

Then at William’s face.

SPENCER
Take your hand off me.

William does not.

For one second, father and son are locked in the doorway.

Then —

A sound outside.

Hooves.

Clear now.

Close enough.

John Samuel looks through the side of the window.

His face drains.

He sees them.

Not shadows.

Men.

Riders coming down.

He turns.

The words come out before he can shape them.

JOHN SAMUEL
Here they come, Grandpap.

The room stops.

The line has been waiting since Episode 1.

Now it arrives like a bell.

EXT. HORNER YARD — DAWN

The riders come into view beyond the yard, emerging from the ridge side.

Not fast.

That is worse.

They are close enough to be recognized.

Jess Wilson is among them.

The borrowed auger hangs from his saddle.

Spencer sees it from the doorway.

His face changes.

Not surprise.

Recognition.

Betrayal.

Humiliation.

Grief for the world that just proved itself dead.

INT. HORNER HOUSE / DOORWAY — CONTINUOUS

William sees Jess too.

WILLIAM
Back.

Spencer does not move.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
Back.

Spencer lowers William’s hand from his arm.

SPENCER
They know me.

William’s voice breaks, but barely.

WILLIAM
That’s why.

Spencer steps into the doorway.

Permelia moves toward him.

PERMELIA
Spencer.

He does not look back.

SPENCER
Stay in.

Mary Ann clutches Christopher.

John Samuel stands frozen.

William moves after Spencer.

EXT. HORNER YARD — DAWN

Spencer steps into the yard.

Rifle low, not raised.

A man trying to keep this from becoming what it already is.

The riders stop short of the house.

Jess’s eyes meet Spencer’s.

Spencer looks at the auger.

Then back to Jess.

SPENCER
You come to return that?

No one laughs.

Jess says nothing.

The second rider shifts in the saddle.

Spencer looks at the others.

Known faces.

Not all.

Enough.

SPENCER (CONT'D)
I know you boys.

That line lands.

A few riders look away.

The young rider cannot hold Spencer’s gaze.

The second rider does.

SECOND RIDER
Then you know why we’re here.

Spencer’s face hardens.

SPENCER
No.

A beat.

SPENCER (CONT'D)
I know what you told yourselves.

The second rider raises his gun.

William steps out of the house.

WILLIAM
Pa!

Spencer turns slightly toward William.

That small turn saves nothing.

The shot cracks.

Fast.

Loud.

Ugly.

Spencer falls.

Not cinematic.

A body losing argument with gravity.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

Mary Ann gasps.

Christopher tries to scream into her hand.

Permelia’s face empties.

John Samuel does not move.

He has stopped being seven.

EXT. HORNER YARD — CONTINUOUS

William sees Spencer fall.

Everything in him wants to go to him.

Instead, he understands the second truth faster than grief can stop him.

They will kill him too.

He turns and runs.

Not toward the house.

Toward the field.

Toward the wooded hillside.

Toward almost.

The riders react.

SECOND RIDER
There!

Jess watches William run.

For one second he does nothing.

Then the second rider spurs forward.

Two others follow.

The young rider stays back.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

Mary Ann sees William running.

MARY ANN
William.

She moves toward the door.

Permelia grabs her.

Hard.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
Let me go.

PERMELIA
No.

MARY ANN
Let me go.

PERMELIA
No.

Mary Ann fights her.

Permelia holds.

Not tender.

Life-saving.

PERMELIA (CONT'D)
They take you too.

Mary Ann stops because the truth is stronger than her body.

EXT. FIELD / POTATO PATCH — DAWN

William runs across open ground.

Boots in wet soil.

Breath tearing.

The wooded hillside ahead.

Close.

Too far.

A shot.

Dirt kicks near his feet.

He keeps running.

Another shot.

He stumbles.

Keeps moving.

The camera does not make it heroic.

It makes it exhausting.

Terrible.

A man measuring life in yards.

EXT. HORNER YARD — CONTINUOUS

Spencer lies near the yard.

Still.

The riders near the house.

Jess remains mounted.

He looks at the doorway.

He sees faces inside.

Mary Ann.

Permelia.

The boys.

He looks away first.

The second rider and others pursue William.

The young rider sits frozen on his horse.

YOUNG RIDER
He’s down.

Jess does not answer.

YOUNG RIDER (CONT'D)
The old man’s down.

Jess looks at him.

JESS
Then don’t say it like news.

EXT. FIELD EDGE — DAWN

William reaches the potato patch.

Almost to the wooded rise.

A rider catches up from the side.

A shot.

William goes down hard.

He tries to rise.

Cannot.

The second rider dismounts.

Another rider joins him.

We do not stay close.

We stay back.

We hear enough.

A dull strike.

A strangled sound.

Another.

Then silence.

The wooded hillside stands just beyond him.

Almost.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

Mary Ann hears the sound more than sees it.

That is worse.

She folds inward.

Permelia still holds her.

John Samuel is at the window.

William is down.

Spencer is down.

The riders are still there.

Christopher cries silently against Mary Ann’s skirt.

John Samuel whispers, not to anyone:

JOHN SAMUEL
He almost made it.

No one answers.

EXT. HORNER YARD — DAWN

The second rider returns toward the house.

One rider stays near William’s body in the field.

Jess dismounts now.

He walks toward the house.

Slow.

The auger hangs from the saddle behind him.

Spencer’s body lies between Jess and the doorway.

Jess steps around him.

That is the worst thing he does in this moment.

Not because it is violent.

Because it is casual.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

Permelia moves Mary Ann and the boys back.

PERMELIA
Away from the door.

Mary Ann can barely stand.

John Samuel does not move.

Permelia turns on him.

PERMELIA (CONT'D)
John Samuel.

He obeys.

Jess appears in the doorway.

He does not enter yet.

He looks into the room.

A house he has seen before.

A family he knows.

A table where they ate.

His eyes go to the plates.

The chicken bones.

The bread.

The molasses.

Then to Permelia.

JESS
Morning.

No one answers.

Permelia holds his gaze.

PERMELIA
You brought my tool?

Jess looks toward the auger outside.

He almost smiles.

Almost.

But her wording stops him.

My tool.

Not Spencer’s.

Not his.

Hers now, because Spencer is dead.

JESS
We need to look around.

PERMELIA
You already did.

A beat.

Jess understands she means before today.

The second rider pushes in behind him.

SECOND RIDER
Move.

Jess steps inside.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

The men search.

Not chaos.

Worse.

Familiarity.

A hand goes to shelves.

A trunk.

A storage corner.

Bedding.

A wrapped paper.

The house is being read by men who know how houses hide things.

Mary Ann clutches Christopher.

John Samuel watches every hand.

Permelia stands like a post driven into the floor.

SECOND RIDER
Where’s the money?

No one answers.

SECOND RIDER (CONT'D)
Gold?

Still nothing.

He looks at John Samuel.

SECOND RIDER (CONT'D)
Boy.

Permelia steps between them.

PERMELIA
Ask me.

The second rider looks her over.

SECOND RIDER
Where is it?

PERMELIA
If we had gold, you think I’d be feeding children beans?

Jess glances at her.

The line lands because it is true.

The second rider does not like being made foolish.

He sweeps a cup from the table.

It hits the floor.

Christopher flinches.

Mary Ann covers him.

EXT. FIELD — SAME

William lies still in the potato patch.

A rider stands over him.

Then turns away.

He believes William is dead.

He does not check carefully.

Or does not care.

The wooded hillside waits beyond.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

The search continues.

A drawer.

A shelf.

A mattress.

Nothing worth the dead.

The young rider stands near the doorway, pale.

He looks at Spencer’s body outside.

Then at the boys.

John Samuel stares back.

The young rider looks away.

Jess notices.

JESS
Outside.

The young rider obeys, grateful.

The second rider finds a small object — maybe Spencer’s coin pouch, maybe a keepsake.

He pockets it.

Permelia sees.

Says nothing.

Her face records it.

That matters.

EXT. HORNER YARD — MOMENTS LATER

The riders begin withdrawing.

Jess is last.

He stops beside Spencer’s body.

For one second, guilt almost becomes action.

It does not.

He mounts.

The auger still hangs from his saddle.

Permelia appears in the doorway behind him.

PERMELIA
Jess.

He turns.

She says nothing else.

She does not need to.

His name is accusation enough.

Jess rides away.

The riders follow.

The yard empties.

Spencer remains.

William remains in the field.

The house remains.

Changed forever.

INT. HORNER HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

The family does not move at first.

The silence after the riders leave is not relief.

It is disbelief.

Then Mary Ann breaks toward the door.

This time Permelia does not stop her.

MARY ANN
William.

She runs.

John Samuel follows.

PERMELIA
John Samuel!

He stops in the yard, trembling.

Permelia points to Christopher.

PERMELIA (CONT'D)
Stay with him.

He wants to run.

He looks at William in the distance.

Then at Christopher.

He goes back.

That choice ages him again.

EXT. HORNER YARD / FIELD — DAWN

Mary Ann runs past Spencer.

She cannot stop.

Not yet.

That guilt will come later.

She runs through wet grass.

Through the field.

Toward William.

Permelia follows slower, because she stops beside Spencer.

She kneels.

Touches his shoulder.

Only once.

No time.

She rises and continues after Mary Ann.

EXT. POTATO PATCH — DAWN

Mary Ann reaches William.

Falls to her knees.

He is not moving.

She puts her hands on him.

Searches.

Face.

Chest.

Mouth.

MARY ANN
William.

Nothing.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
William.

Then —

A breath.

Small.

Awful.

Alive.

Mary Ann freezes.

Then turns toward Permelia.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
He’s breathing.

Permelia arrives.

Looks down.

Her face does not soften.

Because breathing means work now.

Danger.

Decision.

PERMELIA
Can he move?

Mary Ann shakes her head.

MARY ANN
No.

Permelia looks toward the house.

Then the ridge.

Then the wooded hillside.

The house is known.

The field is open.

The riders may return.

PERMELIA
We can’t take him back.

Mary Ann looks at her like she has said something monstrous.

MARY ANN
He’s alive.

PERMELIA
That’s why.

Mary Ann understands.

Hates her for it.

Understands anyway.

EXT. FIELD / HILLSIDE — CONTINUOUS

Permelia looks toward the wooded hillside.

Toward the cave country beyond.

A place not visible from the road.

A place hard to reach.

A place where a dying man might be hidden.

PERMELIA
Get his shoulders.

MARY ANN
What?

PERMELIA
Get his shoulders.

Mary Ann moves.

Permelia takes William’s legs.

William groans.

Barely.

Mary Ann sobs once, then swallows it.

PERMELIA (CONT'D)
Quiet.

Not cruel.

Necessary.

They lift.

The body is too heavy.

The hill is too far.

They begin anyway.

EXT. HORNER HOUSE — SAME

John Samuel stands in the doorway with Christopher behind him.

Christopher cries openly now.

John Samuel watches the women trying to carry William.

He looks at Spencer’s body in the yard.

Then at William.

Then the ridge.

Every direction teaches him something he should never have had to learn.

He whispers:

JOHN SAMUEL
They knew us.

Christopher does not understand.

John Samuel does.

EXT. FIELD TOWARD WOODED HILLSIDE — DAWN

Mary Ann and Permelia drag/carry William toward the trees.

Not gracefully.

Not easily.

Shoes slip.

Dress hems tear.

William’s head lolls.

Mary Ann keeps one hand under it when she can.

Permelia breathes like labor.

Behind them, the house sits exposed.

Ahead, the woods.

No music.

Only breath.

Only effort.

Only the first movement toward the cave.

EXT. RIDGE ROAD — SAME

From far above, Jess Wilson watches.

The riders with him do not.

They think the work is done.

Jess sees the women moving.

Sees William’s body shift.

Realizes.

A beat.

He could speak.

He does not.

The second rider calls from farther ahead.

SECOND RIDER
Jess.

Jess looks once more.

Mary Ann and Permelia disappear into the lower trees with William.

Jess turns his horse and rides after the others.

His silence becomes part of the crime.

EXT. WOODED HILLSIDE — MORNING

The women pull William into shadow.

The house is now partly hidden.

The field visible through trees.

Spencer still in the yard below.

Mary Ann looks back.

Permelia does not.

PERMELIA
Keep moving.

Mary Ann turns forward.

They disappear deeper into the trees.

EXT. HORNER HOMESTEAD — MORNING

Wide.

The yard.

The house.

Spencer’s body.

The chicken dinner table visible through the open door.

The field marked by flattened grass.

The ridge above.

The creek below.

The land holds all of it.

EXT. MODERN WORKSPACE — DAY

Daniel at the table.

No music.

He looks at the phrase:

KILLED BY PARTIES KNOWN TO HIS FAMILY

Then at:

POTATO PATCH

CAVE

EIGHT DAYS

MARY ANN

PERMELIA

CHILD WITNESSES

Daniel’s voice is quiet.

DANIEL (V.O.)
The record can tell me he was killed.

A pause.

DANIEL (V.O.)
Family memory tells me he was found breathing.

Another pause.

DANIEL (V.O.)
The difference between those two things is the cave.

Daniel writes:

THE CAVE IS NOT SAFETY.

THE CAVE IS WHAT THEY HAD LEFT.

Cut back.

EXT. WOODED HILLSIDE — MORNING

Mary Ann and Permelia continue upward.

William between them.

Alive.

Barely.

The screen holds on the struggle.

No heroic angle.

No clean path.

Just two women carrying what the men with guns left behind.
