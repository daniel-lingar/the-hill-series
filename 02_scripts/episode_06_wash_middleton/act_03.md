# Episode 6 — Wash Middleton
## Act 3 — Almost Is the Cruelest Category

**Status:** GAP CLOSED — FIRST FULL SCRIPT PASS

**Evidence Lane:** Wash Middleton and Fate Arnold reprisal material is oral tradition unless independently corroborated. This act dramatizes the danger of acting on partial identification.

---

### SCENE 1 — EXT. RIDGE ABOVE DANCE HOUSE — NIGHT

The dance house is not a palace of evil.

That is what makes it dangerous.

It is light leaking through wall cracks. Fiddle scraped rough. Boots on plank. Women standing near the wall. Children asleep under coats. Men with guns leaning near the door like guns are just another part of clothing.

WASH MIDDLETON lies behind brush on the ridge, watching.

FATE ARNOLD crouches beside him, restless.

A YOUNG UNION SCOUT is behind them, trying to breathe quieter than a young man knows how.

FATE
That him?

Wash does not answer.

FATE (CONT'D)
Wash.

WASH
I heard you.

Below, a bearded man crosses in front of the doorway. He turns his head. The lantern catches cheekbone, beard, hat brim.

The scout leans forward.

SCOUT
Middleton.

Wash grabs his sleeve hard enough to hurt.

WASH
You name him with your mouth again before your eyes do the work, I will drag you back by that sleeve.

SCOUT
Looks like him.

WASH
Looks like is what gets a cousin killed and a neighbor remembered wrong for fifty years.

Fate stares at the doorway.

FATE
He’s in there.

WASH
Maybe.

FATE
Maybe is enough when they did what they did.

WASH
Maybe is enough to watch. It ain’t enough to shoot into a room with children under coats.

Fate’s jaw works.

Below, the music keeps going.

---

### SCENE 2 — INT. DANCE HOUSE — INTERCUT / OBSERVATION

From outside, we see fragments.

A hand taking a cup.

A woman laughing too loud.

A man near the back door speaking close into another man’s ear.

A pistol tucked into belt.

A child rolling over under a coat.

The man who may be Middleton dances once around the floor. Not villainous. Not innocent. Human. Partial. Hard to read through lantern and distance.

The scout whispers again, softer.

SCOUT
If we wait, he leaves.

WASH
If we don’t wait, we may make a widow out of the wrong woman.

FATE
You scared?

Wash looks at him.

WASH
Every man who says he ain’t is either lying or stupid. I try not to be both at once.

Fate almost smiles despite himself. Then the maybe-Middleton laughs below, and Fate’s face closes again.

FATE
I keep seeing the cave.

WASH
You were not in the cave.

FATE
I heard it.

WASH
Hearing ain’t nothing. Hearing ain’t proof.

FATE
You sound like the officer.

WASH
Then the officer got one thing right.

---

### SCENE 3 — EXT. BACK OF DANCE HOUSE — LATER

The observers shift position as carefully as tired bodies can.

Wash moves like he has spent half his life avoiding being seen by men who know how to look.

Fate is rougher. Angrier. The scout nearly slips on loose dirt.

Below, the back door opens.

Two men step out.

One is the bearded man.

The other is older and keeps his face shadowed.

OLDER MAN
You were told not to stay here.

BEARDED MAN
And you were told not to say names near walls.

Wash’s eyes narrow.

Fate grips his pistol.

WASH
(hand on Fate's wrist)
No.

BEARDED MAN
They got papers now?

OLDER MAN
Everybody’s got papers now. Papers don’t ride fast as men.

BEARDED MAN
Horner boy died?

The scout inhales.

Fate’s whole body surges forward.

Wash tackles him into the brush and clamps a hand over his mouth.

Below, the older man stops.

OLDER MAN
Hear that?

The bearded man turns toward the ridge.

Long silence.

A fiddle strikes back up inside.

The older man spits.

OLDER MAN (CONT'D)
Fox maybe.

The men go back in.

Wash lets Fate go.

Fate shoves him off.

FATE
He said Horner.

WASH
A hundred men can say Horner now.

FATE
You heard him.

WASH
I heard a word. I heard fear. I heard guilt maybe. I did not hear a confession.

Fate is shaking.

FATE
What would be enough for you?

Wash looks back down at the house.

WASH
Enough.

---

### SCENE 4 — EXT. RIDGE TRAIL — LATE NIGHT

They withdraw.

Fate hates every step away from the house.

The scout looks back over his shoulder.

SCOUT
We should have done something.

WASH
We did.

SCOUT
We left.

WASH
Not shooting is not nothing.

The scout does not understand yet.

FATE
That’s a coward’s scripture.

Wash stops.

WASH
No. Cowardice would be killing the easiest man in the room and letting our own hunger call it justice.

FATE
They killed Spencer.

WASH
Maybe men in that room did. Maybe men not in that room did. Maybe the one you want most is smart enough to let another man wear his beard in your head.

Fate steps close.

FATE
You saying I don’t know what I know?

WASH
I’m saying knowing is heavier than wanting. If you can’t feel the weight, you ain’t holding knowing yet.

The words hit Fate harder than a shove.

---

### SCENE 5 — INT. UNION COMMAND TENT — PRE-DAWN

Wash, Fate, and the scout return to the tent.

John Turner is there, awake, because sleep has become useless.

The officer stands over the map.

OFFICER
Report.

The scout opens his mouth.

Wash cuts him off.

WASH
Observed gathering at the suspected house. Saw a bearded man matching partial description. Heard the Horner name spoken outside by same or associated man. Could not confirm identity. Women and children present. No action taken.

The officer looks at the scout.

OFFICER
Is that accurate?

SCOUT
Yes, sir.

FATE
It leaves out that he was him.

OFFICER
Could you identify him under oath?

Fate stares.

FATE
Under oath to who? God or paper?

OFFICER
Both, if you have any sense.

Fate looks away.

OFFICER (CONT'D)
Could you?

FATE
I could say I believe it.

John Turner speaks from the corner.

JOHN TURNER
Belief is not nothing.

Everyone turns.

JOHN TURNER (CONT'D)
But it is not the same as proof.

Fate’s disgust is open.

FATE
Your people in the ground and you talk like a clerk.

John Turner stands.

JOHN TURNER
My people in the ground are exactly why I cannot afford to lie with confidence.

Silence.

---

### SCENE 6 — EXT. CAMP / DAWN

Fate stands alone near the horses, furious at sunrise.

Wash joins him.

FATE
You ever lose anybody?

WASH
Yes.

FATE
Then you know.

WASH
No. Loss don’t make men wise by itself. Sometimes it just makes them loud.

Fate looks toward the road.

FATE
They’re going to slip away.

WASH
Maybe.

FATE
There’s that word again.

WASH
It is the word we got.

FATE
I hate it.

WASH
Good. Hate it. Just don’t turn it into a dead body because it embarrassed you.

Fate says nothing.

Across camp, the officer moves the slip for MIDDLETON from HEARD to STRONGER SUSPICION / NEEDS CONFIRMATION.

Not enough.

More than before.

Almost.

Almost is the cruelest category.

**END ACT THREE.**
