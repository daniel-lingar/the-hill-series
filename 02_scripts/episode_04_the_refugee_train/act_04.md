# Episode 4 — The Refugee Train
## Act 4 — The Ledger

**Status:** GAP CLOSED — FIRST FULL SCRIPT PASS

**Evidence Lane:** Dramatized from Union military administration, refugee movement, and Company K context. Ledger scenes are a narrative device for source discipline.

---

### SCENE 1 — EXT. UNION PICKET LINE — LATE MORNING

They see the flag before they see the men.

It does not bring peace. It brings a different kind of fear.

Union SOLDIERS stand at a rough picket line where the road narrows near a rise. Their uniforms are dirty. Their eyes are tired. Their rifles are not lowered.

Eli raises both hands.

ELI
Families. Refugees.

A CORPORAL steps forward.

CORPORAL
How many?

ELI
Less than started.

The corporal hears that. He does not soften.

CORPORAL
Names and places at the table.

Mary Ann looks at Permelia.

PERMELIA
Here it comes.

---

### SCENE 2 — EXT. MAKESHIFT REGISTRATION TABLE — DAY

A board across two barrels. Ink. Ledger. A soldier-clerk with cramped fingers and no patience left for grief he cannot file.

The CLERK writes while a SERGEANT watches.

CLERK
Name.

A woman gives one.

CLERK
County.

She gives it.

CLERK
Loyalty.

She stares at him.

WOMAN
Hungry.

The sergeant exhales.

SERGEANT
Write Union families unless contradicted.

CLERK
That ain’t procedure.

SERGEANT
Procedure can argue with the wagon full of children.

The line moves.

Mary Ann steps up with the children and Permelia.

CLERK
Name.

Mary Ann cannot make her mouth move.

The clerk looks up, annoyed.

CLERK (CONT'D)
Ma’am. Name.

MARY ANN
Horner.

The sergeant’s face changes just enough.

SERGEANT
Horner from Johnson County?

Mary Ann’s eyes sharpen.

MARY ANN
Who is asking?

SERGEANT
Sergeant Daniel Pike. U.S. Army. I am asking because we have Horners on rolls and reports.

Permelia steps closer.

PERMELIA
Reports say what men with ink can stand to write.

The clerk dips pen.

CLERK
Given names.

MARY ANN
Mary Ann.

PERMELIA
Permelia.

JOHN SAMUEL
John Samuel Acord.

The adults turn. He said it before anyone could stop him.

The clerk writes.

CLERK
Acord?

John Samuel nods.

SERGEANT
Acord too.

Mary Ann looks at him.

MARY ANN
What does that mean?

The sergeant does not answer right away.

SERGEANT
It means your family name is not new to this table.

---

### SCENE 3 — EXT. REGISTRATION TABLE — CONTINUOUS

The clerk pulls a second paper from under the ledger. Company notes. Muster fragments. Casualty mentions. Names half official and half rumor.

SERGEANT
Francis Marion Acord.

John Samuel’s face changes. His father’s name lands like a hand on his chest.

JOHN SAMUEL
That was my pa.

SERGEANT
Died of fever at Lewisburg. July.

Christopher does not understand, but he looks at John Samuel because John Samuel has stopped breathing correctly.

CLERK
William Riley Horner.

Mary Ann closes her eyes.

CLERK (CONT'D)
Reported killed by guerrillas. Johnson County.

MARY ANN
Not reported. Killed.

The clerk stops writing.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
Do not hide it behind reported if the paper is sitting in front of the people who carried him.

The sergeant studies her.

SERGEANT
The army writes what it can prove.

PERMELIA
Then write this: he breathed eight days after men left him for dead.

The clerk looks to the sergeant.

SERGEANT
Write family states he survived wounds several days. Hidden by family. Died after.

CLERK
That ain’t standard.

SERGEANT
Neither is the war in this county.

The clerk writes.

Mary Ann watches each letter like it might save something. It does not save William. But it keeps him from being flattened into one line.

---

### SCENE 4 — EXT. CAMP EDGE — AFTERNOON

The refugees sit in the weak shade of tents and wagons.

The official line is over. The unofficial questions begin.

A UNION PRIVATE approaches carefully.

PRIVATE
Ma’am. Was your William in Company K?

Mary Ann looks to Permelia. Permelia gives no permission and no refusal.

MARY ANN
He was.

PRIVATE
My brother knew a Horner. Said Dr. John Turner organized men up there.

Permelia looks at the private fully now.

PERMELIA
Where is Dr. John?

PRIVATE
Don’t know. Some say moving families. Some say trying to get through roads that don’t want him alive.

Mary Ann’s jaw tightens.

MARY ANN
Tell him if you see him that his father is dead.

The private swallows.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
And William.

PRIVATE
Yes, ma’am.

He hesitates.

PRIVATE (CONT'D)
There are names being said. Around camp.

PERMELIA
Names always get brave after the bodies are somewhere else.

PRIVATE
Middleton. Arnold. Wilson. I ain’t saying proof.

Mary Ann hears Jess Wilson like a door opening in the dark.

MARY ANN
Wilson borrowed an auger.

PRIVATE
Ma’am?

MARY ANN
Before. At the house. A neighbor came and borrowed an auger.

Permelia turns toward her. This memory has weight.

PRIVATE
You want me to tell the sergeant?

Mary Ann looks at the ledger table.

MARY ANN
No.

PERMELIA
Mary Ann—

MARY ANN
Not yet. Heard is not nothing. Heard is not proof.

The words surprise even her. They sound like a rule being born.

---

### SCENE 5 — INT./EXT. LEDGER TENT — LATE AFTERNOON

Mary Ann stands at the edge of the ledger tent.

The clerk is blotting pages.

MARY ANN
If I say a name, what happens?

The clerk looks up.

CLERK
Depends who hears it.

MARY ANN
That is not an answer.

CLERK
It is the only honest one.

The sergeant enters behind him.

SERGEANT
A written accusation needs more than grief.

Mary Ann flinches as if struck.

SERGEANT (CONT'D)
I am not calling it false.

MARY ANN
But you are calling it grief.

SERGEANT
I am saying grief sees clear and crooked both. The paper has to know which is which.

Mary Ann looks at the ledger.

MARY ANN
The paper will not have the whole thing then.

SERGEANT
No paper does.

MARY ANN
Then why should I trust it?

SERGEANT
You shouldn’t. You should use it and know its limits.

That is the closest thing to wisdom the army has offered.

Mary Ann nods once.

MARY ANN
Write what can be written. Leave room for what cannot.

---

### SCENE 6 — EXT. UNION CAMP — SUNSET

The refugee train has become part of a larger camp: tents, wagons, animals, wounded men, children asleep sitting up.

John Samuel sits with the ledger image burned into him.

Christopher holds a crust of bread like treasure.

Mary Ann sits beside Permelia.

MARY ANN
Company K.

PERMELIA
What about it?

MARY ANN
The names keep finding each other.

PERMELIA
That is how families work.

MARY ANN
And how targets work.

Permelia does not answer.

Across camp, the clerk turns a page. Ink dries. Names become lines.

The camera moves over the ledger.

HORNER.

ACORD.

COMPANY K.

JOHNSON COUNTY.

KILLED.

REPORTED.

KNOWN.

The paper is not enough.

But it is the first place the pattern can be seen.

**END ACT FOUR.**
