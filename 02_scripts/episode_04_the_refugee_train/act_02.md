# Episode 4 — The Refugee Train
## Act 2 — Leaving the Cave

**Status:** GAP CLOSED — FIRST FULL SCRIPT PASS

**Evidence Lane:** Supported by family tradition and military/refugee context. Specific departure mechanics are dramatized.

---

### SCENE 1 — INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — COLD OPEN CONTINUATION

William is dead.

The cave does not become quiet after death. It becomes too quiet, like even the dripping water is afraid to claim time.

Mary Ann stays beside him, one hand on his sleeve. Permelia stands near the mouth of the cave, looking out at the morning as if the trees owe her an answer.

John Samuel and Christopher sit together. Christopher is too young to understand death as final. John Samuel is old enough to know he does not want to understand it.

MARY ANN
We cannot leave him here like an animal.

PERMELIA
We cannot carry him past riders in daylight.

MARY ANN
Then what are you saying?

PERMELIA
I am saying there are no clean choices left.

Mary Ann looks toward the cave mouth, then down at William.

MARY ANN
He needs a grave.

PERMELIA
He will have one when the living can move without joining him.

That is the first brutality of the day: even mourning has to wait for safety.

---

### SCENE 2 — EXT. CAVE MOUTH / WOODED SLOPE — MORNING

They come out one by one.

Not as a family leaving shelter. As survivors abandoning a place that saved nothing and still kept them alive long enough to know it.

Mary Ann carries the bundle again. Now it holds no food. Only a cup, stained cloth, and what little they dare carry from the cave.

John Samuel looks back.

JOHN SAMUEL
Are we coming back?

Permelia does not answer too quickly.

PERMELIA
Yes.

JOHN SAMUEL
When?

PERMELIA
When we can return as living people instead of more dead ones.

Christopher reaches for Mary Ann’s skirt. She takes his hand.

The hillside is bright, hot, alive. The world has no decency.

They move.

---

### SCENE 3 — EXT. BRUSH TRACE — LATE MORNING

The route is not a road. Roads are for people with permission to be seen.

They follow animal paths, dry creek lines, the back side of ridges. Every snapped twig sounds like a gunshot to them.

John Samuel walks behind Permelia. He keeps turning back.

PERMELIA
Face forward.

JOHN SAMUEL
I’m remembering.

PERMELIA
Remember with your feet moving.

He faces forward.

A distant column of smoke rises from somewhere beyond the trees.

Mary Ann sees it.

MARY ANN
Whose place?

PERMELIA
Don’t guess.

MARY ANN
I need to know.

PERMELIA
No. You need to live long enough to learn.

A sound ahead: wheels.

Permelia raises a hand.

They crouch in brush. Through leaves they see a small group moving below: women, children, an old man, one wounded boy on a mule, a wagon with bedding piled high and too little food.

Not raiders. Refugees.

The old man looks up the slope as if he can feel eyes on him.

OLD MAN
We ain’t hunting nobody. If you’re hid up there, we’re just trying to get to the road.

Permelia does not move.

Mary Ann watches the wagon. A child in it has a fever. Another woman carries a bundle shaped like a baby.

The same disguise. Or maybe an actual baby. War has made even tenderness suspicious.

Mary Ann steps out.

PERMELIA
(low)
Mary Ann.

MARY ANN
We need people.

PERMELIA
People are what came to the house.

MARY ANN
And people are what carried him.

She steps into view.

The old man lowers his hands where she can see them.

OLD MAN
Ma’am.

MARY ANN
Where are you going?

OLD MAN
Wherever men with papers stop asking if we belong to the wrong dead.

Mary Ann does not smile. But the answer is enough.

---

### SCENE 4 — EXT. REFUGEE GROUP / TRACE — MIDDAY

The families merge awkwardly.

Nobody asks names first. Names are dangerous. They ask what can be carried, who can walk, who has water, who is bleeding.

A WOMAN with a sharp face hands Mary Ann a canteen.

SHARP-FACED WOMAN
Don’t drink it fast.

MARY ANN
I know.

SHARP-FACED WOMAN
Everybody knows until they’re thirsty.

Mary Ann drinks small. Passes to the children.

The old man looks at John Samuel.

OLD MAN
You see riders?

John Samuel does not answer.

PERMELIA
He saw enough.

The old man nods. That answer travels without detail.

A TEENAGE BOY with one arm in a sling studies them.

TEENAGE BOY
You from the Horner place?

The world stops.

Mary Ann turns slowly.

PERMELIA
Who asks?

TEENAGE BOY
Nobody bad. I heard—

PERMELIA
Heard is a hungry thing. It eats names and spits out graves.

The boy swallows.

TEENAGE BOY
I only meant there’s talk.

MARY ANN
There will be more.

She keeps walking.

---

### SCENE 5 — EXT. ROAD EDGE — AFTERNOON

The trace widens toward something like a road.

Road means speed. Road means danger. Road means if cavalry comes, there is nowhere to vanish.

The refugee group pauses under trees before stepping out.

The old man counts with his eyes: people, animals, bundles, fear.

OLD MAN
We move in twos and threes. Not a line. Lines look like something to stop.

PERMELIA
Everything looks like something to stop now.

He nods.

OLD MAN
Then we look like we ain’t worth stopping.

Mary Ann looks back one last time toward the hills.

John Samuel sees her looking.

JOHN SAMUEL
He’s back there.

MARY ANN
I know.

JOHN SAMUEL
We’re leaving him.

MARY ANN
We are leaving so somebody can come back and say where he was.

JOHN SAMUEL
That don’t feel different.

MARY ANN
It isn’t yet.

They step onto the road.

---

### SCENE 6 — EXT. ROAD — LATE AFTERNOON

The refugee train is not dramatic from above. It is small. Slow. Vulnerable. A brown line of people carrying what could not be stolen before they fled.

A wagon wheel squeals. A child coughs. Someone mutters a prayer and stops halfway through, as if God might be listening from the wrong side.

Mary Ann walks with her bundle.

John Samuel walks beside her.

JOHN SAMUEL
Are we Union?

Mary Ann glances at him.

MARY ANN
We are alive.

JOHN SAMUEL
That ain’t what they ask.

PERMELIA
Then maybe they ask wrong.

John Samuel keeps walking.

The camera widens. Hills behind them. Road ahead. Cave hidden now by trees and distance.

The dead are behind them.

The war is ahead of them.

There is no direction that does not carry both.

**END ACT TWO.**
