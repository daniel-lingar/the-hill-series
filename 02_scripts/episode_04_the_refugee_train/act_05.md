# Episode 4 — The Refugee Train
## Act 5 — Company K Enters

**Status:** GAP CLOSED — FIRST FULL SCRIPT PASS

**Evidence Lane:** Supported by Company K cluster research. Specific camp events are dramatized to bridge into Episode 5.

---

### SCENE 1 — EXT. UNION CAMP — NIGHT

Night in camp is not rest. It is inventory.

Men count ammunition. Women count children. Clerks count names. The wounded count breaths without meaning to.

Mary Ann cannot sleep. She sits outside a tent with the stained cave cloth in her lap and the ledger table in sight.

A low argument carries from the command tent.

OFFICER (O.S.)
You have three from one set of families in less than a month and you want me to call it scattered misfortune?

SERGEANT (O.S.)
I want you to call it what we can prove.

OFFICER (O.S.)
I can prove dead men.

Mary Ann looks toward the sound.

Permelia emerges behind her.

PERMELIA
You listening to doors now?

MARY ANN
Doors have been listening to us for days.

---

### SCENE 2 — INT. COMMAND TENT — CONTINUOUS

Mary Ann steps into the command tent without permission.

The OFFICER at the table looks up. Maps, reports, a lantern. The sergeant is beside him. The clerk freezes mid-pen stroke.

OFFICER
Ma’am, this is—

MARY ANN
A place where you talk about my dead.

That stops him.

OFFICER
Your name?

MARY ANN
You already wrote it.

The officer studies her. Then gestures to a chair. She does not sit.

OFFICER
We have reports of multiple deaths tied to Union service families out of Johnson County. Horner. Acord. Possibly others. Company K comes up repeatedly.

MARY ANN
Comes up like a cause or like a graveyard?

OFFICER
Like a pattern.

The word lands.

Pattern.

It is not comfort. It is shape.

MARY ANN
Say the names.

SERGEANT
Francis Marion Acord. Fever at Lewisburg.

Mary Ann absorbs for John Samuel.

SERGEANT (CONT'D)
William Riley Horner. Killed in Johnson County by guerrillas or rebel scout, depending on report.

MARY ANN
By parties known to his family.

The clerk looks up.

CLERK
That phrase—

MARY ANN
It belongs somewhere.

OFFICER
We will not write accusation without witness and corroboration.

MARY ANN
I did not ask you to hang a man with ink.

She puts the stained cloth on the table.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
I ask you not to bury the pattern because it is easier than naming the county that made it.

Silence.

---

### SCENE 3 — EXT. CAMP — LATER NIGHT

John Samuel stands outside the tent, listening. He has heard enough to know his father’s name was said. Enough to know William’s name was said. Enough to know adults can make even death sound like work.

Mary Ann comes out.

JOHN SAMUEL
Is Company K why they came?

Mary Ann looks at him.

MARY ANN
It may be part.

JOHN SAMUEL
Part ain’t an answer.

MARY ANN
No. But it is more honest than pretending one answer can hold it.

John Samuel looks toward the command tent.

JOHN SAMUEL
Did my pa die because of it too?

MARY ANN
Your pa died of fever in service.

JOHN SAMUEL
That ain’t what I asked.

Permelia, behind them, hears the boy becoming older than he should.

MARY ANN
Your pa died because the war took men from farms and put them where disease could reach them. William died because men came with guns. Your grandpap died at the door. Those are not the same. But they live in the same house.

John Samuel thinks on that.

JOHN SAMUEL
I hate that house.

PERMELIA
Then don’t build your life in it.

---

### SCENE 4 — EXT. EDGE OF CAMP — PRE-DAWN

A courier rides in hard before dawn.

Horse lathered. Man exhausted. He nearly falls getting down.

A soldier takes the reins. The courier staggers toward the command tent.

COURIER
Dispatch from road north. Families moving. Irregulars active. One doctor asking after Johnson County refugees.

Mary Ann, half asleep under a wagon, sits up.

PERMELIA opens her eyes.

MARY ANN
Doctor?

The courier is pulled into the tent.

Mary Ann gets to her feet.

---

### SCENE 5 — INT. COMMAND TENT — PRE-DAWN

Mary Ann enters again. This time nobody stops her.

The officer reads the courier note.

OFFICER
Dr. John Turner Horner.

The name changes the air.

MARY ANN
Where?

OFFICER
Road east yesterday. Escorting families. Trying to reach this line or another. It is not clear.

PERMELIA
Nothing is clear except dead men.

OFFICER
He may not know.

MARY ANN
Then someone will tell him.

OFFICER
If he comes in, he comes into command first.

MARY ANN
No. He comes to us first.

The officer looks like he might argue. Then does not.

OFFICER
If he arrives, you will be told.

Mary Ann nods.

---

### SCENE 6 — EXT. CAMP / LEDGER TABLE — MORNING

The camp wakes into activity.

The clerk opens the ledger again. New day. Same dead.

John Samuel stands by the table, staring at the open page.

CLERK
Can you read?

JOHN SAMUEL
Some.

The clerk turns the book slightly.

CLERK
That says Acord.

John Samuel looks.

JOHN SAMUEL
That’s me too.

CLERK
Looks like.

JOHN SAMUEL
Does it say I’m alive?

The clerk does not know what to do with that.

CLERK
Not unless somebody writes it.

John Samuel nods as if he has been handed a mission.

He walks away, finds a scrap of paper, and with a dull pencil begins to make marks.

He writes badly, with effort:

HOUSE.

CAVE.

ROAD.

He pauses.

Then writes:

BELIEVE.

---

### SCENE 7 — EXT. COMMAND TENT — DAY

The officer steps out with the sergeant.

OFFICER
Pull every Company K reference we have. Horner. Acord. Boen. Any Johnson County man tied by kin or road.

SERGEANT
That is going to be a mess.

OFFICER
Then sort the mess.

The sergeant nods.

SERGEANT
What are we calling it?

The officer looks out over the camp: refugees, soldiers, children, empty places where dead men should stand.

OFFICER
We are not calling it yet. We are looking at it.

Across camp, Mary Ann watches.

The pattern has entered the record.

Not solved. Not clean. Not even fully named.

But visible.

A soldier walks by calling toward the clerk:

SOLDIER
Company K files. Bring what you got.

The camera pushes into Mary Ann’s face as she hears it.

Company K is no longer background.

It is the map.

**END ACT FIVE.**
