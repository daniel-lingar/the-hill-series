# Episode 4 — The Refugee Train
## Act 3 — Names on the Road

**Status:** GAP CLOSED — FIRST FULL SCRIPT PASS

**Evidence Lane:** Dramatized from refugee movement, Johnson County division, and Company K / Union-linked family context.

---

### SCENE 1 — EXT. ROAD / OPEN GROUND — MORNING

The refugee train moves slower on the road than it did in the trees.

Visibility has a cost. Every rise feels like exposure. Every bend might hold armed men who believe they own the right to ask questions.

Mary Ann walks near the wagon. Her feet are blistered. She does not look down.

The sharp-faced woman, whose name is SARAH, walks beside her now.

SARAH
You got people ahead?

MARY ANN
I had people behind.

Sarah accepts that as an answer.

SARAH
Everybody on this road does.

The old man, ELI, comes back from the front.

ELI
Riders crossed about an hour ahead. Not military. Not clean enough.

PERMELIA
How many?

ELI
Four. Maybe five.

JOHN SAMUEL
Were they the same?

Every adult hears more in the question than a child should know.

ELI
Same as who?

John Samuel looks at Mary Ann.

Mary Ann looks ahead.

MARY ANN
Keep moving.

---

### SCENE 2 — EXT. DRY FORD — MIDDAY

The road dips to a shallow ford. Mud holds prints: horses, wagon wheels, boots.

Too many. Too recent.

Eli kneels, studies tracks.

ELI
They waited here.

SARAH
For who?

ELI
Whoever looked worth frightening.

Permelia sees a scrap of cloth caught on a thorn. Dark stain on it.

She does not touch it.

A YOUNG MOTHER in the wagon begins to cry silently. She recognizes the kind of place this is.

Mary Ann moves to her.

YOUNG MOTHER
I can’t do another stop.

MARY ANN
Then we do this one moving.

YOUNG MOTHER
What if they come?

Mary Ann looks across the ford.

MARY ANN
Then you carry your baby like he is heavier than fear.

The young mother nods.

The train crosses.

John Samuel looks at the prints. He wants them to tell him who. Tracks refuse.

---

### SCENE 3 — EXT. TREE LINE AFTER FORD — AFTERNOON

They make a short camp without calling it camp.

No fire. No spreading out. No talking above the level of breath.

Sarah shares a small piece of corn bread with Christopher.

SARAH
What’s your name?

Christopher looks to John Samuel.

John Samuel answers for him.

JOHN SAMUEL
Christopher.

Sarah waits.

SARAH
And yours?

John Samuel hesitates.

Names have become doors men can kick open.

JOHN SAMUEL
John.

SARAH
Just John?

PERMELIA
Just John is enough today.

Sarah nods and turns away, not offended. She understands new rules.

Mary Ann sits with her back against a tree. She opens the bundle and finds the stained cloth from the cave.

She almost throws it away.

Instead she folds it smaller.

Permelia notices.

PERMELIA
You keeping that?

MARY ANN
I don’t know what else to keep.

PERMELIA
Keep your mouth. Keep the children. Keep the truth where panic cannot make it sloppy.

Mary Ann looks at the cloth.

MARY ANN
Truth is already sloppy.

PERMELIA
No. Pain is sloppy. Truth has to be sorted out later by people brave enough not to decorate it.

---

### SCENE 4 — EXT. ROADBLOCK / FALLEN TREE — LATE AFTERNOON

A tree lies across the road.

Cut, not fallen.

The refugee train stops.

The silence changes. Everyone hears the same thing: this was made by hands.

From the brush, three ARMED MEN step out. Not uniforms. Not strangers.

The leader is middle-aged, sunburned, with a voice too calm.

LEADER
Afternoon.

Nobody answers.

LEADER (CONT'D)
Road’s hard now. Lot of wrong folks moving through.

Eli steps forward a half pace.

ELI
We’re families.

LEADER
Families got names.

Permelia looks at Mary Ann. There it is.

The leader walks along the line, studying faces, bundles, hands.

LEADER (CONT'D)
Where from?

ELI
Up creek.

LEADER
Lots of creeks.

ELI
That’s why I didn’t figure you needed the name.

The leader smiles without warmth.

He stops in front of John Samuel.

LEADER
You know any Horners?

John Samuel’s body goes rigid.

Mary Ann moves before thought. She steps between them with the bundle in her arms.

MARY ANN
He knows hunger and walking. That is enough for one day.

LEADER
I asked the boy.

PERMELIA
And his mother answered.

The leader glances at Permelia.

LEADER
You his mother?

PERMELIA
Today I am whatever keeps grown men from measuring children for graves.

The brush seems to hold its breath.

One of the armed men shifts uncomfortably. He did not come here to be seen clearly.

The leader studies them.

LEADER
Move the tree.

His men hesitate.

LEADER (CONT'D)
Move it.

They pull the tree aside enough for a wagon.

The train passes.

As Mary Ann goes by, the leader leans close.

LEADER (CONT'D)
Road remembers names whether people say them or not.

Mary Ann does not look at him.

MARY ANN
Then the road can answer for itself.

---

### SCENE 5 — EXT. ROAD BEYOND BLOCK — SUNSET

Nobody speaks for a long time after the roadblock.

The silence is not relief. It is accounting.

John Samuel finally speaks.

JOHN SAMUEL
I almost said it.

MARY ANN
But you didn’t.

JOHN SAMUEL
Is that lying?

PERMELIA
No.

JOHN SAMUEL
Is it truth?

Permelia thinks.

PERMELIA
It is survival. Survival and truth are kin, but they are not the same child.

Mary Ann looks at him.

MARY ANN
There will be a time to say names. That was not it.

JOHN SAMUEL
How do you know?

MARY ANN
Because you are still walking.

---

### SCENE 6 — EXT. NIGHT CAMP WITHOUT FIRE — NIGHT

They sleep in a low hollow off the road.

No fire. No talking. Children tucked between bodies for warmth though August still holds heat in the ground.

Mary Ann cannot sleep.

She sees the leader’s face when she closes her eyes. Not because he attacked. Because he could have. Because he knew names were loaded.

Sarah sits beside her.

SARAH
Tomorrow we should meet soldiers if the road holds.

MARY ANN
Which soldiers?

SARAH
The kind with ledgers.

Mary Ann almost laughs. It comes out like pain.

MARY ANN
Ledgers sound peaceful now.

SARAH
Ledgers can kill too.

Mary Ann looks at the children asleep.

MARY ANN
Then we better tell them careful.

The camera lifts above the hollow. Refugees hidden among trees. Road like a pale scar beside them.

Names travel ahead even when mouths stay closed.

**END ACT THREE.**
