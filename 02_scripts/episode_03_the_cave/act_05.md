# Episode 3 — The Cave
## Act 5 — What They Had Left

**Status:** GAP CLOSED — FIRST FULL SCRIPT PASS

**Evidence Lane:** Supported by family tradition that William survived hidden in a cave/overhang for eight days and was cared for by family women until his death. The exact conversations are dramatized.

---

### SCENE 1 — INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — MORNING

Morning enters the cave as a change in gray, not light.

William is awake before anyone else. His eyes are fixed on the stone above him. He is not seeing stone.

Mary Ann wakes to the sound of him trying to breathe around blood and swelling.

MARY ANN
William.

His eyes move to her.

WILLIAM
I was home.

Mary Ann forces herself to hold steady.

MARY ANN
You are with us.

WILLIAM
Pa was at the door.

Mary Ann does not say he is gone. The cave already knows.

PERMELIA stirs nearby. JOHN SAMUEL sits up instantly, as if sleep never had permission to take him.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
Tell Ma—

MARY ANN
She is here.

William’s eyes drag toward Permelia.

WILLIAM
I thought she was in the house.

PERMELIA
I am wherever I have to be.

A small corner of William’s mouth tries to smile. It fails. But everybody sees the attempt.

---

### SCENE 2 — EXT. HILLSIDE ABOVE CAVE — MIDDAY

The day is all heat.

Mary Ann and Permelia sit just outside the cave mouth under leaves, trading air while the children watch William inside.

Below, the valley looks peaceful in a way that feels obscene. Green trees. Pale sky. A line of creek. The home site hidden by distance and ridge.

MARY ANN
How many days?

PERMELIA
Do not count that way.

MARY ANN
I need to know.

PERMELIA
You know enough.

MARY ANN
Eight is what they will say if he lives eight. Three if he lives three. One if he dies today. People will make it neat after.

Permelia turns toward her.

PERMELIA
People who make it neat were not here.

Mary Ann looks toward the cave.

MARY ANN
If he dies in there, do we tell it?

PERMELIA
Yes.

MARY ANN
Even if they say we should let it rest?

PERMELIA
Rest is for the dead. Silence is what the living choose when they are scared.

Mary Ann absorbs that.

Inside the cave, Christopher begins to whimper.

Both women move without speaking.

---

### SCENE 3 — INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — AFTERNOON

Christopher is not crying from fear.

He is crying because William has asked for water and there is almost none left.

John Samuel holds the cup and will not give it up.

JOHN SAMUEL
He asked me.

MARY ANN
Give it here.

JOHN SAMUEL
I can do it.

Mary Ann sees it then: the boy does not want to watch anymore. He wants a task. A task is something a child can mistake for control.

She kneels beside him.

MARY ANN
Slow.

Together they lift the cup.

William swallows one mouthful, then coughs hard. Mary Ann cups cloth against his mouth. Red blooms through it.

John Samuel’s face empties.

WILLIAM
Don’t look away.

Mary Ann looks at him sharply.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
He already saw enough. Don’t make him think seeing is the wrong part.

John Samuel keeps looking, though tears run down his face without noise.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
You hear me, boy?

JOHN SAMUEL
Yes.

WILLIAM
What happened happened because men did it. Not because you saw it.

John Samuel tries to speak. Nothing comes.

PERMELIA
That is enough.

WILLIAM
No. It ain’t.

He fights for breath.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
If you grow and remember me running, remember I was trying to live. If you remember Pa standing, remember he did what he knew. Don’t make one of us clean by making the other dirty.

Mary Ann closes her eyes. She will carry that sentence whether she wants to or not.

---

### SCENE 4 — EXT. CAVE MOUTH — EVENING

A rider appears on the far ridge.

Only a shape at first. Then horse. Then hat. Then gone behind trees.

John Samuel sees him.

He does not shout.

He backs into the cave and taps Permelia’s arm twice, just like she taught him.

The cave shuts itself down.

No movement. No cup. No cough if the body can help it.

Outside, the horse moves somewhere above them.

William begins to choke.

Mary Ann puts her hand under his head. Permelia puts cloth to his mouth. John Samuel puts both hands over Christopher’s mouth before Christopher can cry.

The sound is small, but in the cave small sound becomes a bell.

Hooves stop.

A long silence.

A man coughs outside. Spits.

RIDER (O.S.)
Ain’t nothing down there but rock.

Another voice farther off.

SECOND RIDER (O.S.)
Come on then.

Hooves move away.

No one breathes normally until they cannot hold it anymore.

Christopher gasps and sobs once.

Permelia gathers him against her.

PERMELIA
Not yet. Not yet.

Mary Ann looks at William. The choking has taken more than air from him. He looks farther away.

MARY ANN
Stay.

William’s eyes open.

WILLIAM
I am trying.

---

### SCENE 5 — INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — NIGHT

Night folds over them.

The family is arranged around William like a living wall.

There is no more pretending the cave is a place of rescue. It is a waiting room made of stone.

William’s breath is ragged. Sometimes it stops long enough for everyone to lean forward. Then it returns, meaner than before.

Mary Ann speaks because silence has become too large.

MARY ANN
They will say you were killed by guerrillas.

William’s eyes shift.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
Or rebel scout. Or parties known. Some man with ink will pick words. It will not be enough.

PERMELIA
Words rarely are.

MARY ANN
Then why keep them?

PERMELIA
Because not enough is still more than nothing.

John Samuel listens. This is going somewhere inside him.

WILLIAM
Known.

Mary Ann leans close.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
They were known.

MARY ANN
By who?

William’s mouth moves around names that do not fully form. Pain takes them back.

MARY ANN (CONT'D)
Do not spend yourself on names.

WILLIAM
Names matter.

PERMELIA
So does breath.

WILLIAM
Breath is leaving.

Nobody answers.

He looks toward John Samuel.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
You tell it right.

JOHN SAMUEL
I don’t know right.

WILLIAM
Then tell it careful.

The line settles.

---

### SCENE 6 — EXT. WOODED HILLSIDE — LATER NIGHT

Mary Ann steps out alone.

She stands under black leaves and lets herself bend over without making sound. No wail. No release. Her whole body shakes, but she keeps the noise locked behind her teeth.

The valley is dark.

Somewhere out there are men who will sleep.

Somewhere out there is the house where Spencer fell.

Somewhere behind her, William is still alive and not saved.

Permelia appears at the cave mouth.

PERMELIA
If you break, break here. Then come back in pieces.

Mary Ann wipes her face with both hands.

MARY ANN
I hate them.

PERMELIA
Good. Hate tells the body something happened. Just don’t let it be the only teacher.

MARY ANN
What else is there?

Permelia looks into the dark.

PERMELIA
Witness.

---

### SCENE 7 — INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — PRE-DAWN

The cave is cold now, even in August.

William is asleep or beyond sleep.

Mary Ann sits with his hand in hers. Permelia sits behind the children. John Samuel is awake again. Christopher dreams and jerks.

The first pale edge of morning comes.

William opens his eyes one last time in this episode.

WILLIAM
Still here?

Mary Ann’s face breaks, but her voice does not.

MARY ANN
We’re still here.

William hears it.

The act does not show death.

It shows the living forced to keep watch beside it.

The sound of water dripping returns. One drop. Then another.

Blackout.

**END ACT FIVE.**
