# Episode 3 — The Cave
## Act 4 — The Water Trip

**Status:** GAP CLOSED — FIRST FULL SCRIPT PASS

**Evidence Lane:** Supported by family tradition that William was hidden in a cave/overhang and fed/nursed for eight days. Specific scene mechanics are dramatized from terrain, household survival logic, and the oral account.

---

### SCENE 1 — INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — BEFORE DAWN

The cave does not wake up because the cave never slept.

Water drips somewhere in the dark. Not enough to drink. Enough to count time.

WILLIAM RILEY HORNER lies against folded cloth and stone. His face is swollen. His breath catches in small, ugly pulls, like each one has to fight its way through him.

MARY ANN kneels beside him with a cup held close to his mouth. She does not pour. She waits for the little opening between pain and choking.

PERMELIA watches the mouth of the cave.

JOHN SAMUEL ACORD, seven years old, sits with his knees pulled to his chest. CHRISTOPHER, smaller, has fallen asleep against him. John Samuel’s eyes are open.

Mary Ann tilts the cup.

WILLIAM
(whisper)
Too much.

MARY ANN
It is not too much. It is just water.

WILLIAM
Sounds like the creek.

MARY ANN looks down into the cup. It is nearly empty.

PERMELIA
We are down to what is in that cup.

Mary Ann does not answer. That is answer enough.

Outside, a bird calls once and then stops.

John Samuel looks toward the light line.

JOHN SAMUEL
Is it morning?

PERMELIA
Not enough to trust.

Mary Ann takes a scrap of cloth, wets it with the last of the water, and lays it across William’s mouth.

WILLIAM
Don’t waste it.

MARY ANN
You are not waste.

The line lands harder than comfort. Nobody softens it.

---

### SCENE 2 — EXT. WOODED SLOPE ABOVE THE CAVE — DAWN

Permelia crawls out first.

She does not stand. She listens.

The woods are August-thick. Green heavy with heat. Leaves wet with night, already promising steam. A place that should be alive with noise feels as if everything with sense is holding still.

Mary Ann comes out behind her with a small bundle wrapped in cloth. It is shaped like a baby if you do not look long.

Inside the bundle: a cup, scraps of cloth, a little corn bread wrapped tight, and an empty vessel for water.

Permelia studies it.

PERMELIA
If they see you carrying that, you hold it like it is sleeping.

MARY ANN
If they ask?

PERMELIA
Then you are tired. Tired women do not explain themselves fast.

Mary Ann takes that in. She adjusts the bundle against her chest.

John Samuel appears at the cave mouth.

JOHN SAMUEL
I can go.

MARY ANN
No.

JOHN SAMUEL
I know how not to make sound.

PERMELIA
That is why you stay.

John Samuel stiffens.

PERMELIA (CONT'D)
If riders come close, you do not move him. You do not answer. You put your hand over Christopher’s mouth if you must. You understand me?

John Samuel nods, but he is too small to carry the size of what she said.

PERMELIA (CONT'D)
Say it.

JOHN SAMUEL
I don’t move him. I don’t answer. I keep Christopher quiet.

Mary Ann looks at him. He is still a child. The war does not care.

MARY ANN
We come back.

JOHN SAMUEL
You don’t know that.

Mary Ann looks down, then back at him.

MARY ANN
No. I don’t. But I am saying it so you have something to hold.

---

### SCENE 3 — EXT. GAME TRAIL / BRUSH — MORNING

The women move through brush that catches skirts, ankles, sleeves. August makes every step louder. Bugs lift from the weeds. Sweat darkens cloth before the sun is fully up.

Mary Ann carries the fake baby-bundle like it weighs more than it does.

Permelia keeps a few yards ahead, stopping often, her body becoming part of the trees each time she listens.

Below them, the creek moves over stone.

Then — voices.

Male. Low. Not close enough for words. Close enough for meaning.

Permelia’s hand rises.

Mary Ann freezes.

Two RIDERS pass along a lower trace, partly hidden by leaves. One is young. One has a gray beard. They are not monsters in the saddle. They are men who could borrow a tool, sit at a table, ask after weather.

That makes it worse.

YOUNG RIDER
Somebody had to carry him.

GRAY RIDER
Maybe buzzards did the rest.

The young rider laughs once, not because it is funny, but because fear sometimes wants to sound brave.

Mary Ann’s grip tightens around the bundle.

The young rider turns his head. Almost sees her.

The bundle gives her cover. A woman holding a baby-shaped thing in the August brush belongs to a world men do not always bother to inspect.

Mary Ann lowers her face and rocks the bundle once.

Permelia watches from the trees, ready with a stone in her hand. A stone is not a weapon. It is an answer when no better answer exists.

The riders move on.

The women stay frozen until the woods returns to itself.

MARY ANN
(under breath)
They know he wasn’t dead.

PERMELIA
They know something. That ain’t the same as knowing where.

MARY ANN
It is close.

PERMELIA
Everything is close now.

---

### SCENE 4 — EXT. CREEK EDGE — LATER MORNING

The creek is low but alive. Clear water cutting around rock. Too loud up close. Too exposed from above.

Mary Ann kneels and fills the vessel slow to avoid splash. Her hands shake. She stops them by pressing her palms against the stone until the shaking becomes pain.

Permelia scans the ridge.

PERMELIA
Do not hurry.

MARY ANN
Hurrying is the only thing my body wants.

PERMELIA
Then let your body want wrong. Your hands do what is needed.

Mary Ann fills the vessel. Then the cup. Then wets the cloth. She puts the food into the center of the baby-bundle and wraps it again.

A distant gunshot cracks across the valley.

Both women go still.

A second shot does not come.

Mary Ann looks toward the direction of home.

MARY ANN
There is no home left to shoot at.

PERMELIA
That never stopped men from shooting.

They climb back up.

---

### SCENE 5 — INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — MIDDAY

John Samuel sits exactly where he was told.

One hand is over Christopher’s mouth. Christopher is awake now, crying without sound.

William’s eyes are open.

WILLIAM
They came?

JOHN SAMUEL
No.

William studies the boy. Knows the lie is a gift.

WILLIAM
Good.

Mary Ann and Permelia crawl back in. John Samuel lets go of Christopher, who gulps air and then stops himself before the sob gets big.

Mary Ann unwraps the bundle.

Water. Food. Cloth.

For a second the cave feels rich.

Mary Ann lifts the cup to William.

WILLIAM
Did they see you?

MARY ANN
No.

PERMELIA
They saw a tired woman carrying what they did not want to bother.

William closes his eyes. Shame moves across his face.

WILLIAM
Women shouldn’t have to do this.

MARY ANN
No one should have to do this.

WILLIAM
I ran.

Mary Ann stops.

WILLIAM (CONT'D)
Pa stood. I ran.

John Samuel looks down.

MARY ANN
You lived long enough for us to find you.

WILLIAM
That ain’t brave.

PERMELIA
Brave is a word men use when they ain’t the ones carrying water under trees.

William opens his eyes.

PERMELIA (CONT'D)
You left death where it was. That is not sin.

The cave absorbs it.

William tries to speak and cannot. Mary Ann wipes his mouth.

---

### SCENE 6 — EXT. CAVE MOUTH — LATE AFTERNOON

Heat presses down. The kind of Arkansas August heat that turns shade into only a lesser fire.

John Samuel sits at the entrance now, hidden by rock and vine.

Below, the world continues. Insects. Leaves. Somewhere, a cow bell. Ordinary life committing the insult of going on.

Christopher whispers behind him.

CHRISTOPHER
Are we dead?

John Samuel does not look back.

JOHN SAMUEL
No.

CHRISTOPHER
It feels like it.

John Samuel thinks about this.

JOHN SAMUEL
Dead people don’t have to be quiet.

Christopher takes that as comfort because there is nothing else.

Inside, Mary Ann hums without melody. Not a song. A rhythm for hands that must keep working.

John Samuel looks out at the slope.

He is learning the first rule of the cave: survival is not clean. It is not brave music. It is people doing ugly necessary things and refusing to call them nothing.

---

### SCENE 7 — INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — NIGHT

The water is half gone.

William sleeps in pieces. His breathing is worse.

Mary Ann’s head dips. Permelia catches the cup before it tips.

PERMELIA
Sleep.

MARY ANN
If I sleep, he might—

PERMELIA
He might anyway.

The words are hard. They are also mercy.

Mary Ann looks at William, then at the children.

MARY ANN
I do not know how to be in a world where he dies twice.

PERMELIA
Then be in this one until morning.

Outside, thunder rolls far away, not promising rain.

John Samuel watches both women. He is too young to understand most of the words, but old enough to know a rule is being made.

The cave is not hiding them from grief. It is only slowing grief down enough for hands to do one more thing.

Mary Ann lies down beside William, not touching the wound, only close enough for him to know she is there if some part of him can still know.

William’s hand moves slightly.

Mary Ann takes it.

WILLIAM
(whisper)
Still here?

MARY ANN
We’re still here.

The cave goes dark around that answer.

**END ACT FOUR.**
