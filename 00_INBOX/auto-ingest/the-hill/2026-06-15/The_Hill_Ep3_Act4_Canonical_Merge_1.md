# THE HILL
## Episode 3 — "The Cave"
### Act Four — "The Baby Bundle"
#### Canonical Merge Draft

---

**FADE IN:**

---

### EXT. MULBERRY RIDGE — MORNING

The sun comes up heavy.

Not bright. Not clean. Just hot.

The ridge sweats. The trees hold still. Bugs work the air. Somewhere below, the creek moves over stone like nothing has happened.

A narrow footpath cuts through brush.

MARY ANN HORNER moves along it, slow and careful, carrying a bundle against her chest.

From a distance, it looks like a sleeping child.

A shawl wrapped tight. One hand under it. One hand over it.

Her face does not move.

Behind her, PERMELIA follows with a second bundle.

Smaller.

Tighter.

Neither woman speaks.

They do not walk like mourners.

They walk like women who know a road can watch back.

A crow calls.

Mary Ann freezes.

Permelia stops behind her.

Nothing.

Then another sound.

Leather.

A horse shifting somewhere above the tree line.

Mary Ann lowers her eyes and keeps walking.

Not faster.

Faster would confess.

She hums under her breath.

Not a song anyone would remember. Just noise. A mother's noise. Something ordinary enough to pass under suspicion.

Permelia adjusts her grip on the bundle.

A tin cup inside it gives the smallest clink.

Both women stop breathing.

The horse shifts again.

A MAN'S VOICE carries faint through the trees.

Not clear enough to hear the words.

Only the shape of men talking.

Mary Ann turns slightly, putting her body between the ridge and the bundle.

Permelia whispers.

**PERMELIA**
Do not look up.

Mary Ann does not.

The voices fade.

The horse moves off.

Still, they wait.

One breath.

Two.

Three.

Only then do they move again.

---

### EXT. WOODED SLOPE ABOVE THE CAVE — LATER

JOHN SAMUEL ACORD crouches behind a cedar.

Seven years old.

Too young for what his eyes have learned.

His little brother CHRISTOPHER crouches beside him, trying to copy the silence.

Christopher's knees bounce.

John Samuel puts a hand on them.

Not hard.

Just enough.

Christopher stills.

Below them, Mary Ann and Permelia come through the brush.

John Samuel looks past them.

Checks the ridge.

Checks the hollow.

Checks the trail.

He has been made into a lookout before he can spell the word.

Mary Ann sees him.

She does not smile.

A smile would break something.

She gives one small nod.

John Samuel nods back.

A grown man's nod in a child's face.

---

### INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — CONTINUOUS

The cave is not deep.

It is a wound in the hill.

Rock mouth. Damp floor. A shelf of stone far enough back to hide a body if the trees do their part.

WILLIAM RILEY HORNER lies against folded cloth.

His face is worse than before.

Fever has hollowed him. One eye swollen. Breath shallow. Every sound costs him.

MARY ANN enters with the bundle.

The cave changes around her.

Not safer.

Just less empty.

Permelia enters behind her.

The boys remain near the mouth, where they can hear the woods.

Mary Ann kneels and unwraps the bundle.

Inside:

A jar of water.

Cornbread wrapped in cloth.

A small strip of boiled meat.

Clean rags.

A needle.

A bottle with a few drops of medicine.

William opens his eyes.

He tries to focus.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
Baby?

Mary Ann stops.

Just for a second.

Then she understands.

He has seen the bundle.

**MARY ANN**
No.

She pulls the cloth back farther.

**MARY ANN**
Supper.

William tries to smile.

It fails halfway.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
Fooled me.

**PERMELIA**
It is meant to fool men with rifles. Not you.

Mary Ann dips cloth in water and touches it to his lips.

William drinks like a man ashamed of needing water.

Mary Ann sees the shame.

**MARY ANN**
Do not waste strength apologizing to water.

He swallows.

His hand twitches near the blanket.

Mary Ann takes it.

---

### EXT. CAVE MOUTH — SAME

John Samuel watches the trees.

Christopher whispers.

**CHRISTOPHER**
Is Uncle William going to get up?

John Samuel does not answer.

Christopher looks at him.

**CHRISTOPHER**
John?

John Samuel keeps his eyes on the woods.

**JOHN SAMUEL**
Watch the trail.

**CHRISTOPHER**
I am.

**JOHN SAMUEL**
Then watch it quiet.

Christopher presses his lips together.

A fly lands on his cheek.

He does not slap it.

That is what the cave teaches.

---

### INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — SAME

Mary Ann unwraps William's bandage.

Permelia looks away first.

Not because she is weak.

Because seeing it again is an insult to the body.

Mary Ann does not look away.

She studies the wound with the hard attention of a woman forced to become a nurse because no doctor can safely come.

William groans.

Mary Ann pauses.

**MARY ANN**
I know.

She removes the cloth slowly.

**MARY ANN**
I know.

He grips her wrist.

Weak, but urgent.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
Did they burn it?

Mary Ann looks at him.

**MARY ANN**
What?

**WILLIAM RILEY**
House.

Mary Ann's face tightens.

Permelia watches her.

The answer is not simple. Nothing has been simple since the first shot.

**MARY ANN**
It still stands.

William closes his eyes.

Relief and grief cross him at once.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
Pa?

Silence.

The cave has held that question for days.

Mary Ann looks down at the rag in her hand.

Permelia moves closer.

**PERMELIA**
He died quick.

William opens his eyes.

The words enter him slowly.

Not because he did not know.

Because knowing and being told are not the same wound.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
He was at the door.

**MARY ANN**
Yes.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
He knew them.

Mary Ann does not answer.

That is answer enough.

William's breathing breaks.

He tries to turn his face from them, but pain catches him.

Mary Ann steadies him.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
I ran.

**MARY ANN**
You moved because living moves.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
I left him.

Permelia steps in. Sharp.

**PERMELIA**
No.

William's eyes find hers.

**PERMELIA**
You did not leave him. They took him.

William shakes his head, barely.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
I heard him fall.

Mary Ann's hand stops moving.

That sentence lands in the cave like another body.

Outside, the boys are still.

They heard it too.

Mary Ann forces herself to continue dressing the wound.

**MARY ANN**
Then you heard the truth. He fell. You did not push him.

William's mouth trembles.

He is a soldier.

A son.

A man hidden in a cave by women carrying food under a baby shawl.

All of those things are true.

None of them can save him from himself.

---

### EXT. RIDGE ABOVE CAVE — MIDDAY

A RIDER appears between trees.

Not close.

Close enough.

He stops his horse near the top of the slope.

Looks down.

The cave mouth is hidden by shadow and brush, but the slope has signs if a man knows how to read them.

A broken stem.

A track in soft earth.

A scuffed stone.

John Samuel sees him.

His whole body goes cold.

Christopher starts to turn.

John Samuel grabs his sleeve.

The rider leans forward in the saddle.

Listening.

A second rider appears behind him.

Then a third.

Men, half-shadowed by leaves.

One spits tobacco.

Another points down the slope.

John Samuel's eyes widen.

Inside the cave, Mary Ann hears nothing yet.

John Samuel looks toward the cave mouth.

He cannot call out.

Calling out would bring the men.

He looks at the stone near his foot.

Small.

Flat.

He picks it up.

His hand shakes.

He tosses it toward the cave mouth.

Not hard.

Just enough.

The stone clicks once against rock.

Inside, Permelia's head snaps up.

Mary Ann freezes.

William understands before anyone tells him.

The riders shift above.

One laughs.

Not happy.

Just bored.

That is worse.

---

### INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — CONTINUOUS

Permelia moves to the cave mouth but stays inside the shadow.

Mary Ann gathers the food cloth fast, hiding what she can.

William tries to sit up.

Pain crushes him.

Mary Ann puts a hand on his chest.

**MARY ANN**
No.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
If they come—

**MARY ANN**
No.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
Mary—

She leans close.

Her voice is low enough that only he can hear it.

**MARY ANN**
You do not get to die trying to make men feel brave.

That stops him.

Outside, a horse snorts.

Permelia's face hardens.

She picks up the empty baby shawl.

Wraps it around the jar.

Makes it look full again.

Mary Ann sees what she is doing.

Permelia steps toward the light.

Mary Ann grabs her arm.

**MARY ANN**
No.

**PERMELIA**
If they see somebody, let them see somebody old.

**MARY ANN**
You are not bait.

Permelia looks at her.

**PERMELIA**
Everybody left alive is bait.

She pulls free.

---

### EXT. CAVE MOUTH / SLOPE — CONTINUOUS

Permelia emerges from the brush with the bundle held against her.

Her posture changes.

Shoulders bent. Tired. Harmless.

A grandmother carrying what looks like a child.

The riders see her.

One turns his horse.

**RIDER ONE**
Old woman.

Permelia does not answer.

She steps carefully down the slope, away from the cave mouth.

Drawing their eyes.

Drawing their attention.

John Samuel and Christopher remain hidden behind the cedar.

John Samuel's hand covers Christopher's mouth.

Not cruel.

Necessary.

The riders move a little lower.

**RIDER TWO**
Where you headed?

Permelia keeps walking.

**PERMELIA**
Away from men asking women questions.

Rider One laughs.

Rider Two does not.

**RIDER TWO**
What's in the cloth?

Permelia looks down at the bundle.

Then back at him.

**PERMELIA**
What do you think is in it?

The rider hesitates.

The answer is obvious from a distance.

A baby.

Or something close enough to one that a man would have to admit what kind of man he is to demand it opened.

Rider One spits.

**RIDER ONE**
Let her go.

Rider Two keeps looking.

For one second, the whole world narrows.

The cave.

The boy's hand.

The bundle.

The rider's suspicion.

The heat.

The buried breathing of William Riley Horner.

Then a sound breaks from farther up the ridge.

Another rider calls.

**DISTANT RIDER**
Road!

Rider Two looks back.

The men turn.

Hooves shift.

One by one, they move away through the trees.

Permelia keeps walking until they are gone.

Only then does she stop.

The bundle trembles in her arms.

Not from fear alone.

From the force it took to stand still.

---

### INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — AFTER THE RIDERS

Permelia returns.

Mary Ann takes the bundle from her.

For a moment, neither woman speaks.

Then Mary Ann opens the cloth and checks the jar.

Unbroken.

Still water inside.

Permelia sits down hard against the rock wall.

She looks older than she did that morning.

William watches her.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
I am sorry.

Permelia closes her eyes.

Almost angry.

**PERMELIA**
If one more person apologizes for being alive, I will slap the whole cave.

Christopher lets out a tiny laugh from the mouth.

Too small to be safe.

But it is the first child sound the cave has heard in days.

Mary Ann looks toward him.

She does not scold him.

William hears it.

The laugh does something to his face.

It hurts him and heals him and proves something that cannot last.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
That Christopher?

John Samuel appears at the edge of the light.

Christopher beside him.

Mary Ann starts to wave them back, then stops.

William lifts two fingers.

Barely.

The boys come closer.

Not all the way.

John Samuel stands stiff.

Christopher clings to his sleeve.

William looks at them like he is trying to memorize what his eyes cannot hold.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
You boys mind your mothers.

Christopher nods too fast.

John Samuel does not nod.

He stares.

William sees the question in him.

The one nobody wants to answer.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
You heard them coming?

John Samuel nods.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
You told the house?

Another nod.

William breathes in.

It catches.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
That was good.

John Samuel's face twists.

He has been waiting to be blamed.

Praise wounds him worse.

**JOHN SAMUEL**
They still came.

William closes his eyes.

Mary Ann looks at the boy.

Permelia looks away.

No one has a clean answer.

William opens his eyes again.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
Warning ain't stopping.

John Samuel listens.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
Warning is love moving faster than men.

The boy does not understand all of it.

But the sentence enters him.

It will stay.

---

### EXT. RIDGE ROAD — AFTERNOON

The riders are gone from the slope.

The threat has moved, not ended.

JESS WILSON sits by a low fire a half mile up the ridge with the second rider and two others.

They are not hunting.

They are waiting out the afternoon.

Jess stares into the fire.

The young rider is not with them.

**SECOND RIDER**
You hear he might not be dead?

Jess does not look up.

**JESS**
Who?

**SECOND RIDER**
The son.

Jess still does not look up.

**JESS**
Who said?

**SECOND RIDER**
Nobody.

A beat.

**SECOND RIDER**
Just talk.

Jess looks at him now.

**JESS**
Then stop talking.

The second rider smiles.

**SECOND RIDER**
That worry you?

Jess stands.

**JESS**
Loose talk gets men buried.

**SECOND RIDER**
Thought that was the point.

Jess steps close.

For a moment, violence among the attackers feels possible.

Then he stops himself.

**JESS**
Not by us.

The second rider watches him.

Jess walks away from the fire.

Not redeemed.

Not safe.

Just infected by what he helped do.

The fire burns low behind him.

Below, somewhere through the trees and stone and dark water, the cave holds what Jess Wilson helped put there.

He does not go toward it.

He does not go far enough away.

---

### EXT. MULBERRY RIDGE — LATE AFTERNOON

The heat breaks.

Clouds gather over the hills.

Dark and swollen.

The woods take on a green-black cast.

Thunder rolls far away.

At the homestead, smoke from the chimney rises thin.

No men in the yard.

No cattle lowing.

A house can still stand and be emptied.

---

### INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — EVENING

Rain begins.

At first, it taps leaves.

Then stone.

Then it comes hard.

Water runs down the lip of the overhang and drips near William's feet.

Mary Ann pulls the blanket back.

Permelia shifts the supplies higher.

John Samuel and Christopher press into the dry side of the cave.

The cave is no longer hiding them from the world.

It is taking water.

Mary Ann watches the dirt floor darken.

A thin stream finds its way along the rock.

Toward William.

She moves him.

He cries out.

The sound is not loud.

That makes it worse.

Mary Ann freezes.

Then keeps moving him.

**MARY ANN**
I know.

She and Permelia lift him by inches.

The kind of work that has no witness except God and stone.

William's breath breaks into pieces.

Christopher covers his ears.

John Samuel does not.

He watches every second.

---

### INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — DEEP NIGHT

William wakes with pain.

This time stronger.

His body arches slightly.

Mary Ann holds him down.

**MARY ANN**
William.

He cannot hear her over the pain.

Permelia moves to help.

William's hand grabs at nothing.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
Pa—

The word tears through him.

Not Mary.

Not water.

Pa.

Mary Ann holds his face.

**MARY ANN**
Look at me.

William's eyes search.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
I left him.

Mary Ann freezes.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
I ran.

**MARY ANN**
You lived.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
I ran.

Permelia kneels close. Sharp and direct.

**PERMELIA**
Hear me.

He tries.

**PERMELIA**
Your father was dead before your feet hit the field.

Mary Ann flinches at the bluntness.

Permelia does not soften.

**PERMELIA**
You did not leave him alive.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
I left him.

**PERMELIA**
You left death where it was.

A beat.

**PERMELIA**
That is not sin.

William cannot hold the words.

But something in him hears them.

His body eases slightly.

Mary Ann keeps her hand on his cheek.

**MARY ANN**
Stay with me.

William looks at her.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
I tried.

**MARY ANN**
Try again.

He breathes.

Once.

Again.

Trying.

---

### EXT. CAVE MOUTH — STORM / CONTINUOUS

The rain does what mercy could not.

It covers them.

Washes away tracks.

Blurs the path Mary Ann and Permelia took.

Erases small signs.

For a few hours, the storm is the only protection they have that costs nothing.

---

### INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — LATE NIGHT

William lies higher now, against the back wall.

His face shines with fever.

Mary Ann wrings water from a cloth.

Permelia sits beside the boys.

Christopher sleeps against her side.

John Samuel stays awake.

He stares at the mouth of the cave where rain falls silver in the dark.

William speaks.

Barely.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
Mary.

She leans in.

**MARY ANN**
I am here.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
If John comes—

She knows which John.

Dr. John Turner.

Brother. Son. Soldier. Doctor. All of it.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
Tell him I waited.

Mary Ann swallows.

**MARY ANN**
You can tell him.

William's eyes move to her.

Not accusing.

Just tired of lies that are meant to comfort.

Mary Ann cannot hold his gaze.

Permelia watches from the shadows.

William breathes.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
Tell him I tried.

Mary Ann takes his hand.

**MARY ANN**
I will tell him what happened.

William's fingers tighten slightly.

**WILLIAM RILEY**
All of it?

Mary Ann looks toward the cave mouth.

The rain.

The boys.

The bundle.

The ridge.

The house.

The father at the door.

The son in the dirt.

The men known and unnamed.

Her voice changes.

Not louder.

Heavier.

**MARY ANN**
All I can carry.

William closes his eyes.

That is enough for now.

---

### INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — PRE-DAWN

The lantern is almost out.

The rain has slowed to a drip at the overhang's edge.

John Samuel sits awake.

Barely.

His head nods once.

He catches himself.

Permelia is against the wall, eyes heavy but not fully closed.

**JOHN SAMUEL**
You can sleep.

**PERMELIA**
No.

**JOHN SAMUEL**
I'll watch.

**PERMELIA**
No.

**JOHN SAMUEL**
I watched before.

**PERMELIA**
And I wish you hadn't.

He takes that in.

Quietly.

**JOHN SAMUEL**
But I did.

Permelia looks at him.

No child left in that answer.

**PERMELIA**
Wake me if you hear anything that is not the creek, bugs, wind, him breathing, or your brother dreaming.

John Samuel nods.

A sacred assignment.

Permelia closes her eyes.

Not fully asleep.

But close.

John Samuel listens.

Creek.

Bugs.

Wind.

William breathing.

Christopher dreaming.

He learns the catalog of what is allowed.

Then —

A sound.

Not creek.

Not bugs.

Not wind.

Not William.

Not Christopher.

A low movement outside.

He reaches for Permelia.

Touches her arm.

She wakes instantly.

No startle.

Ready.

He points.

Permelia crawls toward the mouth.

Mary Ann sees.

Covers William with darker cloth.

Christopher wakes and clamps his own sleeve between his teeth before anyone tells him.

That small automatic act devastates Mary Ann.

---

### EXT. CAVE MOUTH — PRE-DAWN / CONTINUOUS

Permelia looks out.

A shape moves in brush.

Close.

Too close.

She grips the knife.

The shape steps into faint gray.

A hog.

Wild or half-wild.

Snuffling.

Not men.

Permelia exhales silently.

The hog moves off.

She almost laughs.

Not because it is funny.

Because her body cannot hold more fear.

She goes back inside.

---

### INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — CONTINUOUS

Everyone looks at her.

**PERMELIA**
Hog.

Christopher releases his sleeve.

**CHRISTOPHER**
Can we eat it?

The question enters the cave the way only a child's question can.

Practical. Innocent. Impossible.

A child who has learned to bite down on fear asking, with complete sincerity, if the thing that scared them might feed them.

Mary Ann almost cries.

Not at the question.

At what the question proves.

That Christopher now lives in a world where those two things are the same calculation.

Threat or food.

Fear or hunger.

He is six years old.

John Samuel answers softly.

**JOHN SAMUEL**
Not that one.

Christopher nods.

He accepts this the way he has learned to accept everything in the cave.

Without being told why.

Permelia looks at Mary Ann.

Mary Ann looks at the boys.

She does not speak.

Neither does Permelia.

There is nothing to say about what the children are becoming that would help the children stop becoming it.

**PERMELIA**
Sleep if you can.

No one really can.

But the cave settles again.

---

### INT. CAVE / OVERHANG — DAWN

Another morning.

The second full day beginning.

Light enters the cave mouth.

Gray first.

Then pale.

No warmth yet.

William is still alive.

Barely.

Mary Ann's face is gray with exhaustion.

Permelia's eyes are red.

John Samuel has not slept enough to dream.

Christopher sleeps with his sleeve still damp in his mouth.

Mary Ann feels William breathe.

Then looks at Permelia.

**MARY ANN**
We can't keep doing this.

Permelia looks at William.

At the children.

At the cave mouth.

**PERMELIA**
We already are.

Not encouragement.

Not despair.

The truth.

The cave holds them all.

For one more day.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

---

**END OF ACT FOUR.**
